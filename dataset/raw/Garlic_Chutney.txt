Name: Garlic Chutney
Tamil Name: பூண்டு சட்னி
Category: chutney
Sub Category: chutney
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Garlic Chutney, also known as Poondu Chutney or Lehsun Ki Chutney, is a spicy and pungent South Indian condiment made with garlic, dry red chillies, coconut and tamarind. The chillies are soaked, ground with garlic into a paste, and then slow-cooked until the raw smell disappears. A simple tempering of mustard and curry leaves finishes this bold, flavour-packed chutney that pairs beautifully with idli, dosa and upma.

Ingredients:

* Garlic cloves (பூண்டு - பூண்டு): 20 big cloves (or 25 to 30 small cloves)
* Dry red chillies / Kashmiri red chillies (வற்றல் மிளகாய் - வற்றல் மிளகாய்): 10
* Tamarind (புளி - புளி): 1 tsp
* Jaggery powder (வெல்லம் பொடி - வெல்லம் பொடி): ½ tsp
* Gingelly oil / Sesame oil (நல்லெண்ணெய் - நல்லெண்ணெய்): 1½ tbsp (divided)
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 1 cup (for soaking) + ¼ cup (for cooking)

For Tempering:
* Gingelly oil (நல்லெண்ணெய் - நல்லெண்ணெய்): 1 tbsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): 1 tsp
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 sprig

Steps:

1. Boil 1 cup water. Add dry red chillies and let them soak for 10 to 15 minutes.
2. Cool completely, then drain the red chillies. Reserve the soaked water.
3. To a mixer jar, add drained red chillies, garlic cloves and tamarind.
4. Add a little of the reserved soaked water and grind to a slightly fine paste.
5. Heat ½ tbsp gingelly oil in a small kadai. Add the chilli-garlic paste.
6. Rinse the mixer jar with ¼ cup water and add it to the kadai.
7. Mix well. When it starts to boil, cook partially covered (it splutters a lot).
8. Open, give a quick mix, then cook covered partially again. Repeat until the raw smell of garlic leaves — about 10 minutes. The chutney will thicken.
9. Add required salt and jaggery. Mix well.
10. Add ½ tbsp gingelly oil, mix and cook for 2 more minutes.
11. For tempering, heat 1 tbsp gingelly oil in a tadka pan. Add mustard seeds and curry leaves. Let them splutter.
12. Add tempering to the chutney. Give a quick mix and switch off.
13. Serve hot with idli, dosa, upma, oothapam or vada.

Tips:

* Use Kashmiri red chillies for a bright red colour and milder heat level.
* Gingelly oil is preferred for this recipe as it gives a nice flavour and balances the spice.
* Jaggery balances the spice level and adds a subtle sweetness.
* Cooking until the raw smell of garlic completely disappears is crucial — do not rush this step.
* This chutney can be stored in the refrigerator for 3 to 4 days.
* For a spread-style chutney, cook until very thick and oil separates.

Variations:
Garlic Tomato Chutney (roasted), Poondu Thogayal (coarse), Dry Garlic Chutney (Lehsun ki Chutney with desiccated coconut)

Commonly served with:
Idli, Dosa, Oothapam, Upma, Pongal, Vada, Parathas, Roti

Name: Pudalangai Kootu
Tamil Name: புடலங்காய் கூட்டு
Category: kootu-poriyal
Sub Category: kootu
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Pudalangai Kootu is a traditional Tamil Nadu style snake gourd kootu made with moong dal and fresh coconut-cumin paste. This mild, healthy and comforting dish is a regular in Tamil households and is usually served as a side dish with rice, sambar and rasam.

Ingredients:

* Snake Gourd / Pudalangai (புடலங்காய் - புடலங்காய்): 1 medium (300-350 grams)
* Moong dal (பாசிப்பருப்பு - பாசிப்பருப்பு): ¼ cup
* Turmeric powder (மஞ்சள் தூள் - மஞ்சள் தூள்): ¼ tsp
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste

For Grinding:
* Grated coconut (தேங்காய் துருவல் - தேங்காய் துருவல்): ½ cup
* Green chilli (பச்சை மிளகாய் - பச்சை மிளகாய்): 1-2
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): 1 tsp

For Tempering:
* Coconut oil or gingelly oil (தேங்காய் எண்ணெய் அல்லது நல்லெண்ணெய் - தேங்காய் எண்ணெய் அல்லது நல்லெண்ணெய்): 2 tsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ½ tsp
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): 1 tsp
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 sprig
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): 1 pinch

Steps:

1. Rinse moong dal and pressure cook with ¾ cup water and turmeric powder for 2-3 whistles until soft. Mash lightly and set aside.
2. Wash, peel the outer skin of snake gourd, remove the inner seeds and chop into small pieces.
3. In a pan or pressure cooker, add chopped pudalangai, little water and cook until soft (2 whistles if using pressure cooker).
4. Grind grated coconut, green chilli and cumin seeds into a smooth paste with little water.
5. Add the cooked moong dal and ground coconut paste to the cooked snake gourd. Mix gently.
6. Add salt and simmer for 3-4 minutes until the kootu thickens slightly.
7. Heat oil in a small tadka pan. Add mustard seeds and let them crackle.
8. Add urad dal and fry till golden. Add curry leaves and asafoetida. Pour this tempering over the kootu.
9. Mix well and serve hot.

Tips:

* Choose tender snake gourd for better taste.
* Do not add too much water while cooking the vegetable.
* You can add a pinch of jaggery for a mild sweetness (optional).
* This kootu tastes best when served warm with rice.

Variations:
Pudalangai Kootu with chana dal, Snake gourd poricha kootu, Mixed vegetable kootu

Commonly served with:
Steamed rice, Sambar, Rasam, Curd rice

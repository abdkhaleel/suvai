Name: Ragi Koozh
Tamil Name: கேழ்வரகு கூழ்
Category: drinks
Sub Category: koozh
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Fermenting Time: 8 hours
Servings: 2
Description:
Ragi Koozh, also known as Kezhvaragu Koozh or Aadi Koozh, is a traditional South Indian fermented millet drink made with finger millet (ragi), raw rice and buttermilk. It is a healthy cooling food prepared especially during the Tamil month of Aadi. The fermentation process develops natural probiotics and a pleasant sour flavour. Nutritious, filling and refreshing, it is an excellent breakfast or summer meal that aids digestion and provides sustained energy.

Ingredients:

* Ragi flour / Finger millet flour (கேழ்வரகு மாவு - கேழ்வரகு மாவு): ¼ cup
* Raw rice (அரிசி - அரிசி): 2 tbsp
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 1½ cups (for cooking) + ½ cup (for dissolving ragi)
* Buttermilk (மோர் - மோர்): ½ cup
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): a pinch

For Garnish:
* Small onions / Shallots (சின்ன வெங்காயம் - சின்ன வெங்காயம்): finely chopped
* Green chilli (பச்சை மிளகாய் - பச்சை மிளகாய்): 1 (chopped)
* Coriander leaves (கொத்தமல்லி இலை - கொத்தமல்லி இலை): 1 tbsp (chopped)
* Raw mango (மாங்காய் - மாங்காய்): 3 tbsp (chopped, optional)
* Ginger (இஞ்சி - இஞ்சி): 1 tsp (grated, optional)

Steps:

1. Pulse 2 tbsp raw rice in a blender until it reaches a coarse, rava-like texture. Do not grind into fine powder.
2. In a bowl, mix ¼ cup ragi flour with ½ cup water. Whisk thoroughly until smooth and completely lump-free.
3. Bring 1½ cups water to a rolling boil in a heavy-bottomed pot.
4. Add the coarsely ground rice and cook on medium heat for 10 minutes until soft.
5. Lower the flame and slowly pour the ragi slurry into the pot while stirring continuously.
6. Cook on low-medium heat for 15 minutes, stirring constantly. The mixture will thicken and turn glossy.
7. Add a pinch of salt and mix well.
8. Cover the pot and allow the mixture to ferment at room temperature for 8 hours (preferably overnight).
9. After fermentation, the mixture becomes slightly sour, airy and thick.
10. Take the required portion of fermented ragi base. Add ½ cup buttermilk and mix well until smooth and drinkable.
11. Garnish with chopped shallots, green chilli, coriander leaves, raw mango and grated ginger.
12. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

Tips:

* Fermented ragi koozh thickens significantly — always dilute with buttermilk just before serving.
* Adding salt after cooking helps better fermentation.
* For babies, skip fermentation and garnishes. Serve fresh, warm ragi porridge instead.
* Use freshly prepared homemade ragi powder for better texture and flavour.
* Cook on medium flame and keep stirring to avoid getting burnt at the bottom.
* The consistency should be semi-solid or medium liquid — adjust buttermilk accordingly.
* You can add chopped items while cooking itself, or at the end for crunch.
* Ragi Koozh is best consumed fresh on the day of fermentation.

Variations:
Sweet Ragi Koozh (with jaggery), Ragi Kanji (thin porridge), Instant Aadi Koozh (without fermentation), Ragi Koozh with Pickle

Commonly served with:
Uppu Mangai (Raw Mango Pickle), Tangy Kuzhambu, South Indian Breakfast, Aadi Festival Meal

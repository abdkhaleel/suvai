Name: Peanut Chutney
Tamil Name: வேர்க்கடலை சட்னி
Category: chutney
Sub Category: chutney
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 5 minutes
Servings: 3
Description:
Peanut Chutney, also known as Groundnut Chutney, Verkadalai Chutney or Palli Chutney, is a flavourful nut-based side dish from Andhra and other Southern parts of India. Roasted peanuts are ground with onions, garlic, chillies, tamarind and jaggery to create a creamy, nutty and spicy chutney. It is quick to make and tastes amazing with idli, dosa, punugulu or vada.

Ingredients:

* Roasted peanuts / Groundnuts (வேர்க்கடலை - வேர்க்கடலை): ½ cup (unsalted, skinned)
* Onion (வெங்காயம் - வெங்காயம்): 1 small, chopped
* Garlic (பூண்டு - பூண்டு): 3 to 4 cloves
* Dry red chillies (வற்றல் மிளகாய் - வற்றல் மிளகாய்): 4 to 5
* Tamarind (புளி - புளி): 1 small piece (or 1 tsp tamarind pulp)
* Jaggery (வெல்லம் - வெல்லம்): 1 tsp (optional)
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): ¼ to ½ cup (for grinding)

For Tempering:
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): 1 tbsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ½ tsp
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): ½ tsp
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 sprig
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): 1 pinch

Steps:

1. If using raw peanuts, dry roast them on low flame for 4 to 5 minutes until aromatic and golden. Remove skins if needed.
2. Add roasted peanuts, onion, garlic, dry red chillies, tamarind, jaggery and salt to a blender jar.
3. Add ¼ cup water and grind to a smooth or slightly coarse paste. Add more water as needed for consistency.
4. Transfer the peanut chutney to a serving bowl.
5. For tempering, heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them crackle.
6. Add urad dal and fry until golden.
7. Add curry leaves and asafoetida. Fry for a few seconds until leaves are crisp.
8. Pour the tempering over the chutney and mix well.
9. Serve with idli, dosa, vada, paniyaram or punugulu.

Tips:

* Always use roasted peanuts for this chutney. Raw peanuts will not give the right flavour or texture.
* If using store-bought roasted peanuts, check that they do not contain corn syrup or additives.
* Adjust the number of red chillies based on your spice preference.
* The chutney has a creamy yet nutty mouthfeel — similar to a spicy peanut sauce.
* You can skip onion and garlic for a simpler version, or add grated coconut for extra richness.
* This chutney stays good in the refrigerator for 2 to 3 days.

Variations:
Tomato Peanut Chutney, Coconut Peanut Chutney, Spicy Peanut Chutney, Mint Peanut Chutney

Commonly served with:
Idli, Dosa, Vada, Paniyaram, Punugulu, Upma, Pongal, Parathas

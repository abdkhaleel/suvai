Name: Kozhukattai
Tamil Name: கொழுக்கட்டை
Category: sweets
Sub Category: kozhukattai
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: medium
Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Servings: 12 kozhukattais
Description:
Kozhukattai, also known as Mothagam or Vella Kozhukattai, is a traditional South Indian steamed sweet dumpling made especially for Ganesh Chaturthi as a favourite offering to Lord Ganesha. It consists of a soft rice flour outer shell filled with a sweet coconut-jaggery stuffing (pooranam). The dumplings are shaped into small cups, filled and then steamed until tender. Making the perfect dough without cracks is the key to beautiful kozhukattais.

Ingredients:

For Dough:
* Rice flour / Idiyappam flour (அரிசி மாவு / இடியப்பம் மாவு - அரிசி மாவு / இடியப்பம் மாவு): ½ cup
* Sesame oil (நல்லெண்ணெய் - நல்லெண்ணெய்): 1 tsp
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): as needed
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): a pinch

For Coconut Pooranam (Filling):
* Grated coconut (தேங்காய் துருவல் - தேங்காய் துருவல்): 1 cup
* Jaggery (வெல்லம் - வெல்லம்): ¾ cup (powdered)
* Cardamom (ஏலக்காய் - ஏலக்காய்): 2 (powdered)
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 2 tbsp

Steps:

1. For the dough, bring water to a boil with 1 tsp sesame oil and a pinch of salt. Let it boil for a minute.
2. Add the boiling water little by little to the rice flour. Use a spatula to mix. You may need lots of water — boil enough.
3. Once it becomes warm, knead to a smooth, soft dough. Keep covered with a wet cloth.
4. Divide into equal-sized balls and keep covered.
5. For thenga pooranam, place grated coconut, jaggery and cardamom powder in a pan with 2 tbsp water.
6. Mix on medium flame for 5 minutes. When it starts leaving the sides of the pan, switch off. Do not overcook or it will become hard.
7. Take one dough ball. Knead again with moist or oiled hands to prevent cracks.
8. Shape into a small cup with your fingers — thin walls, thicker base.
9. Fill with 1 to 1½ tsp of coconut pooranam.
10. Pinch the edges together and shape into a modak form (pointed top) or round ball.
11. Repeat and finish all kozhukattais. Keep covered until steaming.
12. Boil water in a steamer (idli pot). Grease the steaming plate lightly with oil.
13. Arrange the kozhukattais on the plate with some space between them.
14. Steam for 5 minutes or until the outer layer turns slightly shiny and cooked through.
15. Remove carefully and serve warm.

Tips:

* Using processed idiyappam flour gives the best results — better than store-bought "kozhukattai flour."
* Keep the dough covered at all times with a wet cloth to prevent drying and cracking.
* Before shaping each kozhukattai, knead briefly with wet hands. If too dry, wet hands and knead again.
* Do not shape and keep for long — steam immediately after shaping to prevent cracking.
* The pooranam should not be overcooked — switch off as soon as it leaves the sides of the pan.
* You can reduce jaggery quantity to suit your taste.
* If the dough cracks while shaping, it needs more moisture — add a few drops of hot water and knead.
* Kozhukattais are best eaten fresh on the same day.

Variations:
Ulundu Kozhukattai (with urad dal filling), Ammini Kozhukattai (tiny, no filling), Kara Kozhukattai (savoury), Paal Kozhukattai (in sweet milk)

Commonly served with:
Ganesh Chaturthi Prasad, Festival Offerings, Neivedyam, Breakfast

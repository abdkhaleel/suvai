Name: Rava Laddu
Tamil Name: ரவா லட்டு
Category: sweets
Sub Category: laddu
Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Servings: 18 laddus
Description:
Rava Laddu, also known as Sooji Ladoo or Rava Ladoo, is a simple and delicious South Indian sweet made from roasted semolina, coconut, sugar and ghee. These round fudge balls have a wonderful textural contrast — crunchy from the roasted rava and nuts, yet melt-in-the-mouth from the ghee and sugar. A must-have during Diwali and other festivals, Rava Laddu can be prepared in under 30 minutes with basic pantry ingredients.

Ingredients:

* Rava / Sooji / Semolina (ரவா - ரவா): 2 cups (coarse)
* Desiccated coconut (காய்ந்த தேங்காய் துருவல் - காய்ந்த தேங்காய் துருவல்): 1 cup
* Sugar (சர்க்கரை - சர்க்கரை): 1¾ cups
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): ½ cup
* Ghee (நெய் - நெய்): ¼ cup
* Cashews (முந்திரி பருப்பு - முந்திரி பருப்பு): 2 tbsp
* Raisins (திராட்சை - திராட்சை): 2 tbsp
* Cardamom powder (ஏலக்காய் பொடி - ஏலக்காய் பொடி): ½ tsp

Steps:

1. Heat ¼ cup ghee in a kadai. Add cashews and raisins. Roast until nuts turn golden brown. Remove and set aside.
2. In the same ghee, add 2 cups rava. Roast on low flame until aromatic and slightly golden in colour. Do not brown.
3. Add 1 cup desiccated coconut and roast for another 2 minutes until aromatic.
4. Transfer the roasted rava and coconut to a large plate and cool slightly.
5. In the same kadai, take 1¾ cups sugar and ½ cup water. Stir and boil until one-string sugar syrup consistency is reached.
6. Add the roasted rava and coconut to the syrup. Mix well.
7. Cook until the mixture just comes to a boil. Do not overcook or the laddus will turn hard.
8. Transfer the mixture to the large plate and cool slightly.
9. Once the mixture cools, it will be moist and easy to shape. Add roasted nuts and ½ tsp cardamom powder. Mix well.
10. Shape into laddus of your desired size while the mixture is still warm. If too dry, add 2 tbsp milk and mix.
11. Cool completely and store in an airtight container.

Tips:

* Use coarse rava for a nice texture. Fine rava can also be used but gives a different mouthfeel.
* Roast rava on low flame to prevent browning — it should turn light golden, not brown.
* Remove roasted rava promptly into a cool bowl to stop the cooking process.
* The sugar syrup should reach one-string consistency — too thin and laddus won't hold; too thick and they become hard.
* Do not let the mixture cool completely before shaping — it hardens and won't bind.
* Adding coconut enhances flavour and keeps the laddus moist.
* If using fresh grated coconut, reduce the quantity and finish the laddus within a day.
* Rava Laddu stays good for up to a month in an airtight container.

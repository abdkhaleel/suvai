Name: Aval Upma Recipe | Poha Upma
Tamil Name: அவல் உப்புமா (Aval Uppuma)
Category: breakfast
Sub Category: tiffin
Diet: vegetarian
Difficulty: 
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Servings: 3 to 4

Description:
Aval Upma (Poha Upma) is a South Indian version (Tamil style) of poha upma made with thick flattened rice (poha/aval), some veggies and other basic ingredients. It is an easy and tasty breakfast recipe. This poha upma includes mustard seeds, chana dal, urad dal, ginger, green chilies, curry leaves, onion, cashews, asafoetida, coconut and turmeric powder.

Ingredients:
Prep ingredients for aval upma:
- 2 cups thick poha (parched rice or flattened rice)
- ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
- ½ teaspoon sugar or add as per taste
- salt as required

Veggies:
- 1 medium potato
- 1 small to medium carrot
- 1/3 cup green peas – fresh or frozen

Tempering and other ingredients:
- 2 tablespoon oil
- 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
- 1 teaspoon chana dal (split husked bengal gram)
- 1 teaspoon urad dal (split husked black gram)
- 10 to 12 cashews or 1 tablespoon peanuts
- 1 dried red chili
- 1 or 2 green chilies – chopped
- ½ inch ginger – finely chopped
- 8 to 10 curry leaves
- 1 pinch asafoetida (hing)
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped or 1/3 cup finely chopped onion
- 2 tablespoon grated coconut (optional)
- 2 to 3 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves
- 1 small lime or lemon for serving

Steps:
1. Steam veggies if adding (potato, carrot, peas). Peel and chop potato and carrot. Or cook veggies directly with the tempering.
2. Rinse 2 cups thick poha in water in a colander or strainer for a couple of times gently so that it softens. Add ½ tsp turmeric powder, sugar and salt to the poha. Mix gently. Keep aside.
3. In a pan, heat 2 tbsp oil and add 1 tsp mustard seeds.
4. When the mustard seeds crackle, add 1 tsp chana dal, 1 tsp urad dal and 10 to 12 cashews (or peanuts).
5. Stir and saute till both the dals and cashews turn golden.
6. Now add 1/3 cup finely chopped onion, 1 tsp finely chopped ginger, 1 tsp chopped green chilies, 1 dry red chili, 8 to 10 curry leaves and a pinch of asafoetida.
7. Stir and saute till the onions turn translucent.
8. Add the veggies and stir very well.
9. Then add the poha. Stir and mix gently. Check the taste and add more salt or sugar if required.
10. Cook on a low flame for 3 to 4 minutes. You can also cover the pan and allow the upma to steam for 3 to 4 minutes on a low flame.
11. Lastly add 2 to 3 tbsp chopped coriander leaves and mix gently. You can also add 2 to 3 tbsp grated coconut at this step.
12. You can also drizzle some lime or lemon juice and mix. Or serve with lime slices.
13. Serve poha upma hot or warm with lemon or lime slices or a lemon pickle.

Tips:
- While rinsing the poha, make sure to move it with your hands gently so that it just softens.
- You can make this upma with or without veggies.
- Chana dal, urad dal and asafoetida are optional.
- Instead of cashews, you can also add 1 tablespoon peanuts.
- You can increase the quantity of green chilies for spicier upma.
- Garnish with grated fresh coconut and drizzle some lemon juice on top.

Variations:
- Make with or without vegetables.
- Skip chana dal, urad dal or asafoetida.
- Use peanuts instead of cashews.

Commonly served with:
lemon or lime slices or lemon pickle

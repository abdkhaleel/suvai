Name: Sundal
Tamil Name: சுண்டல்
Category: snacks
Sub Category: sundal
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Soaking Time: 10 hours
Servings: 3
Description:
Sundal is a healthy, protein-rich South Indian snack made with boiled legumes tempered with mustard, urad dal, red chillies, curry leaves and asafoetida, finished with fresh grated coconut. It is a staple during Ganesh Chaturthi, Navratri and other festivals. Chana Sundal (Kondakadalai Sundal) is the most popular variety, but it can be made with any legume.

Ingredients:

* White chickpeas / Chana / Kondakadalai (கொண்டைக்கடலை - கொண்டைக்கடலை): ½ cup (dry)
* Grated coconut (தேங்காய் துருவல் - தேங்காய் துருவல்): ¼ cup
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): ⅛ tsp (divided)
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste

For Tempering:
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): 1 tsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ½ tsp
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): 1 tsp
* Dried red chillies (வற்றல் மிளகாய் - வற்றல் மிளகாய்): 2
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 8 to 10 leaves

Steps:

1. Wash and soak chana overnight (8 to 10 hours). Next day, drain and wash once more.
2. Pressure cook the soaked chana with salt and just enough water to immerse it for 4 to 5 whistles on medium flame.
3. Drain the cooked chana well and set aside.
4. Heat oil in a kadai. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
5. Add urad dal and fry till golden brown.
6. Add dried red chillies, curry leaves and a pinch of asafoetida. Sizzle for a few seconds.
7. Add the cooked chana to the tempering. Sprinkle the remaining asafoetida.
8. Stir continuously for 2 minutes until any remaining moisture evaporates.
9. Add grated coconut and fry for 1 more minute.
10. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Tips:

* Adding asafoetida at two stages — one while tempering and one raw with the chana — gives distinct flavour and aids digestion.
* A drizzle of raw coconut oil (cold pressed virgin oil) at the end adds amazing flavour.
* Do not overcook the chana — it should hold its shape and not turn mushy.
* For festivals, avoid adding onion. For everyday snacking, you can add chopped onion after tempering.
* You can replace red chillies with green chillies for a fresher taste.
* You can grind green chilli and coconut together and add at the end instead of tempering with grated coconut.
* Sundal can also be made with green gram (moong dal), black chickpeas (kala chana), peanuts or rajma.

Variations:
Kondakadalai Sundal, Pattani Sundal (Green Peas), Verkadalai Sundal (Peanut), Pachai Payaru Sundal (Green Gram), Navratri Sundal Varieties

Commonly served with:
Tea, Coffee, Festival Prasad, Navratri Neivedyam, Ganesh Chaturthi Offerings

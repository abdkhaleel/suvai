Soft Idli Recipe - How to Make Soft Idlis

Ingredients:
- 1 cup regular rice + 1 cup parboiled rice (or 2 cups idli rice or 2 cups parboiled rice)
- ½ cup (120 grams) urad dal – whole or split (husked black gram)
- ¼ cup (20 grams) thick poha (flattened rice)
- ¼ teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
- 2 cups water – for soaking rice + poha
- 1 cup water – for soaking urad dal + methi
- ½ cup water (or as required) – for grinding urad dal
- ¾ to 1 cup water (or as required) – for grinding rice
- 1 teaspoon rock salt or sea salt (edible and food grade)
- Oil – as required to grease idli moulds
- 2 to 2.5 cups water – for steaming

Soaking:
1. Pick and rinse the regular rice and parboiled rice well.
2. Rinse the poha and add it to the rice. Add 2 cups water. Mix well, cover and soak for 4 to 5 hours.
3. In a separate bowl, rinse urad dal and fenugreek seeds a couple of times. Add 1 cup water and soak for 4 to 5 hours.

Grinding:
1. Drain the urad dal (reserve the soaking water). Grind urad dal + fenugreek seeds with ¼ cup reserved water, then add another ¼ cup and grind until the batter is smooth, fluffy and light.
2. Transfer urad dal batter to a large bowl.
3. Drain the rice + poha. Grind in batches with ¾ to 1 cup water to a smooth but slightly grainy consistency (like fine rava). Add water as needed.
4. Mix both batters thoroughly.

Fermentation:
- Add 1 teaspoon rock salt or sea salt and mix very well.
- Cover loosely and keep in a warm place for 8 to 12 hours (or until the batter doubles in volume). In cold climates, it may take 12–24 hours.

Making Idlis:
1. Heat 2 to 2.5 cups water in an idli steamer, pot or pressure cooker until it comes to a light boil.
2. Lightly grease the idli moulds with oil.
3. Gently stir the fermented batter. Pour batter into the moulds.
4. Place the mould stand in the steamer or pressure cooker (do not put the whistle).
5. Steam for 12 to 15 minutes or until idlis are cooked (toothpick inserted should come out clean and idlis feel springy).
6. Let them rest for 2–3 minutes, then gently remove with a spoon.

Your soft and fluffy idlis are ready.

Name: Jangiri
Tamil Name: ஜாங்கிரி
Category: sweets
Sub Category: jangiri
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: hard
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Soaking Time: 1 to 2 hours
Servings: 20 jangiris
Description:
Jangiri, also spelled Jhangri, is a classic South Indian sweet made from urad dal batter piped into intricate flower-like patterns, deep-fried and soaked in fragrant sugar syrup. Often confused with Jalebi, Jangiri is distinct — it uses urad dal instead of maida, giving it a soft, spongy interior with a light crispy exterior. The bright orange colour, floral aroma and juicy sweetness make it a showstopper at festivals and family gatherings.

Ingredients:

For Jangri Batter:
* Whole urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): 1 cup
* Idli rice (இட்லி அரிசி - இட்லி அரிசி): 2 tbsp (optional, for crispness)
* Rice flour (அரிசி மாவு - அரிசி மாவு): 1 tbsp (or cornstarch, if needed)
* Orange food colour (ஆரஞ்சு உணவு வண்ணம் - ஆரஞ்சு உணவு வண்ணம்): ⅛ tsp
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): ¼ tsp
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): as needed (very little, for grinding)
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): for deep frying

For Sugar Syrup:
* Sugar (சர்க்கரை - சர்க்கரை): 3 cups
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 1 cup
* Cardamom powder (ஏலக்காய் பொடி - ஏலக்காய் பொடி): 1 tsp
* Lemon juice (எலுமிச்சை சாறு - எலுமிச்சை சாறு): 1 tsp
* Orange food colour (ஆரஞ்சு உணவு வண்ணம் - ஆரஞ்சு உணவு வண்ணம்): ⅛ tsp
* Rose essence (ரோஜா மணம் - ரோஜா மணம்): few drops (optional)

Steps:

1. Rinse urad dal (and idli rice if using) twice. Soak in water for 1 to 2 hours.
2. Drain thoroughly and let sit for 5 minutes to remove excess water.
3. Transfer to a blender or wet grinder. Sprinkle very little water at a time and grind to a thick, smooth paste like medu vada batter.
4. The batter should be very smooth and light. To test consistency, drop a spoonful in water — it should float.
5. Transfer to a bowl. Whip the batter with your hand or a wooden spoon in one direction for at least 10 minutes to aerate it.
6. Add orange food colour and salt. Mix well. If batter is too thin, add 1 tbsp rice flour.
7. For sugar syrup, take 3 cups sugar and 1 cup water in a wide pan. Bring to a boil.
8. Cook until half-string consistency — when stretched between fingers, it forms a string but won't stand stiff. Switch off.
9. Add cardamom, lemon juice, food colour and rose essence. Keep syrup warm.
10. Fill a piping bag or zip-lock cover with the batter. Cut a small hole at the tip.
11. Heat oil in a flat-bottomed pan. The oil should be hot but not smoking.
12. Pipe the batter in circular, flower-like patterns directly into the oil.
13. Fry until both sides are slightly crisp but colour has not darkened too much. Flip carefully.
14. Drain and immediately add to the warm sugar syrup. Soak for 3 to 5 minutes per batch.
15. Remove from syrup and arrange on a plate. Pour remaining syrup over all jangiris.
16. Serve at room temperature.

Tips:

* The batter consistency is crucial — too thin and jangiris won't hold shape; too thick and they won't be soft inside.
* Whipping the batter for 10 minutes aerates it, making the jangiri crispy outside and juicy inside.
* Use a flat-bottomed pan for frying so the jangiris have space to spread.
* Oil temperature is very important — too hot and they will brown quickly without cooking inside; too cool and they will absorb oil.
* Sugar syrup should be warm, not hot, when soaking the jangiris.
* Lemon juice prevents the syrup from crystallizing.
* Use a skewer or wooden chopstick to flip the jangiris gently.
* Jangiris stay soft and juicy for many hours when soaked properly.

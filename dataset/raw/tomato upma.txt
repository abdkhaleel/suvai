Name: Tomato Upma
Tamil Name: தக்காளி உப்புமா (Thakkali Uppuma)
Category: breakfast
Sub Category: tiffin
Diet: vegetarian
Difficulty: 
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 

Description:
Tomato upma is tangy, spicy and tasty upma variety with full on flavors from tomatoes and spices. Makes for an easy and quick breakfast on busy days. It can also be served as an evening tea time snack. The tang from the tomatoes perks up the overall flavor. This is a slightly spicy version. Tomato upma is best served hot drizzled with a bit of ghee on top.

Ingredients:
- 1 cup fine rava (sooji or semolina or cream of wheat)
- 2 tablespoon ghee or oil
- 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1 teaspoon urad dal (husked split black gram)
- 1 teaspoon chana dal (husked split bengal gram)
- 1 or 2 dry red chilies – broken and deseeded
- 1 pinch asafoetida (hing)
- 50 grams onion or 1 medium onion, chopped or ⅓ cup chopped onions
- 150 grams tomatoes or 2 medium to large tomatoes or ¾ cup chopped tomatoes
- 1 or 2 green chilies – chopped
- ½ inch ginger – chopped or 1 teaspoon chopped ginger
- 6 to 7 curry leaves – chopped
- ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder
- ¼ to ½ teaspoon red chilli powder – can also add vangi bath powder or bisi bele bath powder
- 2.5 cups water
- salt as required
- 1 to 2 teaspoon ghee (clarified butter) for drizzling on top – optional

Steps:
1. Heat a pan or kadai first. Add 1 cup rava or cream of wheat.
2. On a low heat, begin to roast the rava. Stir often while roasting the rava. The rava or sooji grains should become fragrant and start to look dry, separate and crisp. Don’t brown the rava. This roasting takes around 3 to 4 minutes.
3. Switch off the heat and then add the roasted rava to a plate and keep aside.
4. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil on low to medium-low heat.
5. Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds. When you hear the crackling sound of mustard seeds, reduce the heat to a low.
6. Add 1 teaspoon cumin seeds, 1 teaspoon chana dal, 1 teaspoon urad dal. Stir and saute them on a low heat till both the urad dal and chana dal become golden.
7. Now add 1 or 2 dry red chilies and a pinch of asafoetida (hing). Stir and mix well.
8. Add ⅓ cup of chopped onions. Saute the onions till they turn translucent.
9. Then add 1 or 2 green chilies (chopped), ½ inch ginger (chopped), 6 to 7 curry leaves (chopped). Saute for half a minute.
10. Add ¾ cup of chopped tomatoes. Stir and mix well.
11. Add ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder and ¼ to ½ teaspoon red chilli powder. Mix very well and saute the tomatoes till they soften.
12. Next add 2.5 cups of water. Add salt as per taste. Check the taste of the water and it should be slightly salty.
13. On a medium to high heat, let it come to a rolling boil.
14. When the water comes to a rolling boil, lower the flame to its lowest. Then add the rava in 4 to 5 batches. Stir and mix very well after each batch.
15. Cover and allow the tomato upma to steam for 2 to 3 minutes on low heat.
16. Then switch off the heat. Lastly add about 2 tablespoons of chopped coriander leaves. Stir and mix again.
17. Drizzle ½ teaspoon of ghee on each serving and serve tomato upma hot.

Tips:
- Use good quality fresh rava. Use the fine textured rava like Bombay rava.
- Ensure that the rava is roasted well and evenly.
- For some crunch you can add cashews or roasted peanuts to the dish.
- Feel free to adjust the spices and seasonings as required.
- This recipe can be scaled as per your needs.

Variations:
- For a less spicy version, skip the red chilli powder.
- You can also add ¼ to ½ teaspoon vangi bath powder or bisi bele bath powder instead of red chilli powder.

Commonly served with:
coconut chutney, lemon pickle or lemon wedges (drizzled with a bit of ghee on top)

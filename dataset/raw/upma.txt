Name: Upma Recipe | Rava Upma (Suji Upma)
Tamil Name: உப்புமா (Uppuma)
Category: breakfast
Sub Category: tiffin
Diet: vegetarian
Difficulty: 
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Servings: 3

Description:
Upma, also known as rava upma or suji upma, is a traditional South Indian breakfast made with roasted semolina. The rava is cooked with mustard seeds, lentils, curry leaves, onions, ginger and green chilies to make a soft, savory dish with mild spice. This upma recipe follows a simple home-style method. It is usually served hot with coconut chutney, lemon wedges or pickle. Upma is a classic South Indian breakfast made with rava (suji or semolina), lentils, nuts, herbs and spices.

Ingredients:
For roasting rava:
- 1 cup rava – finer variety (sooji or cream of wheat or semolina)

Other ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) or oil
- 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
- ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1 teaspoon chana dal (split and husked bengal gram)
- 1 teaspoon urad dal (split and husked black gram)
- 10 to 12 cashews – optional
- ⅓ cup or 1 medium-sized onion – finely chopped
- 1 teaspoon or 1 green chilli – chopped
- 1 teaspoon or 1 inch ginger – finely chopped
- 1 sprig curry leaves or 10 to 12 curry leaves
- 2.5 cups water
- salt as required
- ½ to 1 teaspoon sugar or add as required, optional
- 2 to 3 tablespoons coriander leaves – chopped (cilantro)

Steps:
1. Finely chop 1 medium sized onion, 1 to 2 green chillies and 1 inch ginger and some coriander leaves. Set aside the remaining ingredients.
2. Heat a pan or kadai first. Add rava or cream of wheat.
3. Begin to roast the rava. Stir often while roasting the rava. The rava or sooji grains should become fragrant and start to look dry, separate and crisp. Don’t brown the rava.
4. Switch off the flame and then add the roasted rava in a plate and keep aside.
5. In a pan, heat ghee or oil. Add the mustard seeds.
6. When you hear the crackling sound of mustard seeds, add the cumin seeds along with chana dal and urad dal.
7. Fry till they begin to brown a bit or get lightly golden.
8. Immediately add cashews and begin to fry. By the time, the cashews get golden the lentils will also get golden.
9. Now add the finely chopped onions. Saute the onions till they become translucent.
10. Then add the chopped green chilly, ginger, curry leaves. Sauté for a few seconds.
11. Then add 2.5 cups water, sugar and salt as required. Mix well and check the taste of water. It should be a bit salty but not too much.
12. On a medium to high flame, heat the water and let it come to a rolling boiling.
13. When the water comes to a rolling boil, lower the flame to its lowest. Then add the rava in 4 to 5 batches with a spoon.
14. Once you add the rava, mix and stir immediately. This way keep on adding and stirring the rava up to the last batch.
15. Quickly stir and mix well. Cover and allow the rava upma to steam for 2 to 3 minutes on a low heat.
16. Then switch off the flame. Lastly add chopped coriander leaves. Mix again.
17. Serve upma with coconut chutney or lime slices or lime pickle.

Tips:
- Roast the rava on low to medium-low heat, stirring often. The grains should look separate and give a light toasted aroma.
- Add the rava to hot boiling water in batches, mixing well after each addition to avoid lumps.
- For soft texture use about 1 cup rava to 2½ to 3 cups water. The exact amount depends on the quality and texture of the rava.
- You can make upma with either ghee or oil. Ghee gives better flavor.
- Upma is best served hot or warm.

Variations:
- Add cashews or peanuts for crunch.
- You can skip sugar.

Commonly served with:
coconut chutney, lemon wedges or lemon pickle

Name: Thattai
Tamil Name: தட்டை
Category: snacks
Sub Category: thattai
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: medium
Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Servings: 35 pieces
Description:
Thattai, also known as Thattai Murukku or Thattu Vadai, is a crispy, crunchy South Indian deep-fried snack made from rice flour dough flattened into thin discs. Flavoured with soaked chana dal, urad dal, sesame seeds and curry leaves, these flat crackers are a festival favourite during Diwali and Gokulashtami. Each thattai is individually pressed and fried to golden perfection.

Ingredients:

* Rice flour (அரிசி மாவு - அரிசி மாவு): 2 cups (roasted, sieved)
* Urad dal flour (உளுந்து மாவு - உளுந்து மாவு): 3 tbsp (roasted, sieved)
* Fried gram dal flour / Pottukadalai flour (வறுத்த கடலை மாவு - வறுத்த கடலை மாவு): 2 tbsp
* Chana dal (கடலை பருப்பு - கடலை பருப்பு): 1 tbsp (soaked for 1 hour)
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): 1 tbsp (soaked for 1 hour, optional)
* Butter (வெண்ணெய் - வெண்ணெய்): 2 tbsp (softened)
* Red chilli powder (மிளகாய் தூள் - மிளகாய் தூள்): 1 tsp
* Sesame seeds (எள் - எள்): 1 tbsp
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): ¼ tsp
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 tbsp (finely chopped)
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): as needed
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): for deep frying

Steps:

1. Soak chana dal and urad dal in water for 1 hour. Drain and set aside.
2. Dry roast rice flour for 2 minutes on medium flame without changing colour. Sieve into a mixing bowl.
3. Add urad dal flour, fried gram dal flour, soaked chana dal, sesame seeds, red chilli powder, asafoetida, chopped curry leaves, salt and softened butter.
4. Mix everything well first. Then add water little by little to form a smooth, soft dough.
5. Pinch small portions and roll into gooseberry-sized balls. Keep covered to prevent drying.
6. Grease a butter paper or zip-lock cover with oil. Place a ball and fold the sheet over.
7. Using a flat-bottomed bowl or your fingers, press gently into a thin, even disc.
8. Prick the disc with a fork to prevent puffing while frying.
9. Heat oil on medium heat. Carefully slide the thattai into the hot oil.
10. Fry 3 to 4 at a time on medium-low flame. Flip and fry until golden on both sides.
11. Continue frying until the bubbling sound completely ceases, then cook 1 more minute for extra crispiness.
12. Drain on paper towels. Cool and store in an airtight container.

Tips:

* Press the thattai as thin and even as possible — the thinner, the crispier.
* The dough should be slightly loose, not too dry, or it will crack while pressing.
* Chop curry leaves very finely so they don't interrupt while pressing.
* Oil temperature is crucial — too low and thattai will be soggy; too high and it will brown too quickly.
* Do not make too many layers in the oil — 2 circles per thattai is enough.
* You can add roasted peanuts for extra crunch and flavour.
* Thattai stays crisp for 2 to 3 weeks in an airtight container.

Variations:
Pepper Thattai, Peanut Thattai, Grated Coconut Thattai, Garlic Thattai

Commonly served with:
Tea, Coffee, Filter Coffee, South Indian Mixture

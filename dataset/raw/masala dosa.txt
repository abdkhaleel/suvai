Name: Masala Dosa Recipe (Traditional)
Tamil Name: மசாலா தோசை (Masala Dosai)
Category: breakfast
Sub Category: tiffin
Diet: vegetarian
Difficulty: 
Prep Time: 9 hours
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Servings: 20 masala dosa

Description:
Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian crepe made from a fermented rice and lentil batter, filled with a spiced potato and onion filling. This recipe shows how to make light, crisp dosa with a soft center, similar to the hotel-style version. While it takes some planning, the steps are straightforward and the result is worth the effort. Masala dosa are crispy, soft, savory and healthy crepes made with a rice and lentil batter. The dosa are gluten-free and dairy-free, with a sturdy yet soft texture that’s great for holding a hearty homemade potato filling.

Ingredients:
For Dosa Batter:
- 1.5 cups idli rice (330 grams) – parboiled rice or regular rice
- ½ cup urad dal (125 grams) – husked whole or split black gram
- ¼ cup thick poha (35 grams) – flattened rice
- 1 tablespoon chana dal – husked and split bengal gram
- 20 fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
- 1 cup water for soaking urad dal
- 2 cups water for soaking rice
- 1 cup water for grinding rice & poha
- ½ cup water for grinding dal
- ½ teaspoon rock salt or sea salt (add as required)

For Potato Masala Filling:
- 4 medium potatoes (300 grams) – boiled and chopped or crumbled
- 2 tablespoons oil
- ½ teaspoon mustard seeds
- 1 tablespoon chana dal (optional, soaked in hot water for 30 minutes)
- 1.25 cups thinly sliced onions (about 2 medium-large onions)
- 1 teaspoon finely chopped ginger
- 8 to 10 curry leaves
- 1 teaspoon chopped green chillies (1-2 green chilies)
- ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1 pinch asafoetida (hing) – optional
- ½ cup water
- 3 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
- 2 tablespoons cashews – optional
- ¼ to ½ teaspoon sugar – optional
- Salt as required

Steps:
1. Rinse and soak urad dal, chana dal and fenugreek seeds in 1 cup water for 4-5 hours.
2. Rinse rice a couple of times. Rinse poha once or twice and add to the rice. Soak both together in 2 cups water for 4-5 hours.
3. Drain the lentils and grind them with ½ cup water to a light and fluffy batter. Remove to a large bowl.
4. Drain rice and poha well. Grind with 1 cup water to a fine grainy consistency (like fine rava). 
5. Mix both batters together in the bowl. Add ½ teaspoon rock salt and mix very well. Cover and let it ferment for 8-9 hours or overnight.
6. Boil potatoes until fork tender. Peel and chop or crumble them.
7. Heat oil in a pan. Fry cashews (optional) and keep aside. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
8. Add chana dal and sauté till golden. Add sliced onions, curry leaves, green chillies and ginger. Sauté till onions turn translucent.
9. Add turmeric powder and hing. Mix well. Add ½ cup water and simmer for 2-3 minutes.
10. Add boiled potatoes, salt and sugar (optional). Mix and cook on low flame for 3-4 minutes until the mixture thickens. Add chopped coriander leaves and fried cashews. Keep aside.
11. Stir the fermented batter gently. Heat a cast iron or non-stick tawa on medium heat.
12. Pour a ladle of batter on the hot tawa and spread it thinly in a circular motion to make a crisp dosa.
13. Drizzle some oil or ghee on the dosa and around the edges. Cook till the base turns golden and crisp.
14. Place 2-3 tablespoons of potato masala in the center. Fold the dosa over the filling.
15. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Tips:
- The batter consistency should be medium thick – slightly thinner than idli batter.
- Fermentation time depends on the weather. In warm climates it may take 8 hours; in cold climates it may need 12-14 hours.
- For extra crisp dosa, use a well-seasoned cast iron tawa.
- You can make plain dosa from the same batter without filling.
- Leftover batter can be stored in the refrigerator for 1-2 days.

Variations:
- Add grated coconut or finely chopped carrots to the potato filling.
- Make it spicier by increasing green chillies or adding red chilli powder.
- Use ghee instead of oil for richer taste.

Commonly served with:
coconut chutney, tomato chutney, onion chutney and sambar

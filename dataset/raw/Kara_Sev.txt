Name: Kara Sev
Tamil Name: கார சேவ்
Category: snacks
Sub Category: sev
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Servings: 2 cups
Description:
Kara Sev is a traditional South Indian spicy snack made from gram flour (besan), rice flour and a blend of spices including crushed black pepper, cumin and garlic. It is crunchy, light and aromatic with a unique flavour that sets it apart from murukku and ribbon pakoda. Kara Sev is a Diwali essential and can be eaten plain or added to South Indian mixture. The garlic and ajwain flavour gets enhanced the day after making.

Ingredients:

* Gram flour / Besan (கடலை மாவு - கடலை மாவு): 1 cup
* Rice flour (அரிசி மாவு - அரிசி மாவு): 3 tbsp
* Fried gram flour / Pottukadalai flour (வறுத்த கடலை மாவு - வறுத்த கடலை மாவு): 2 tbsp
* Hot oil (காய்ந்த எண்ணெய் - காய்ந்த எண்ணெய்): 1½ tbsp (for dough)
* Red chilli powder (மிளகாய் தூள் - மிளகாய் தூள்): ¾ tsp (or 1 tsp crushed pepper)
* Garlic paste (பூண்டு விழுது - பூண்டு விழுது): 1 tsp
* Ajwain / Carom seeds (ஓமம் - ஓமம்): 1 to 1½ tsp (or cumin seeds)
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): ¼ tsp
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): ½ tsp (adjust to taste)
* Baking soda / Cooking soda (சமையல் சோடா - சமையல் சோடா): 1 pinch (optional)
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): as needed (for dough)
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): for deep frying

Steps:

1. Powder 2 tbsp roasted gram (pottukadalai) to a fine powder in a mixer.
2. Sieve together gram flour, rice flour, fried gram flour and a pinch of baking soda into a large mixing bowl.
3. Add red chilli powder, ajwain, asafoetida, salt and garlic paste.
4. Add 1½ tbsp hot oil to the flour mixture. Mix well.
5. Pour water little by little and make a stiff dough. It should not be sticky or too crumbly.
6. Sticky dough makes oily kara sev; too stiff and crumbly makes hard kara sev.
7. Choose a murukku plate with thick holes for making kara sev.
8. Grease the murukku mould and transfer half the dough into it.
9. Heat oil in a kadai on medium heat. Test by dropping a small piece of dough — it should rise without browning quickly.
10. Hold the murukku maker 2 to 3 inches above the oil surface. Press gently and squeeze the sev across the pan.
11. When the sev is about 3 inches long, use a spoon to gently break it. The light dough breaks easily and falls into the oil.
12. Do not add too much at once — overcrowding reduces oil temperature and makes the sev absorb oil.
13. Fry on medium heat until golden and crisp, stirring often.
14. Drain on a steel colander or kitchen tissues.
15. Cool kara sev completely before transferring to an airtight steel or glass jar.

Tips:

* The dough should be stiff but smooth — sticky dough makes oily sev; crumbly dough makes hard sev.
* Adding fried gram flour gives kara sev a light, airy texture.
* Only a pinch of baking soda is needed — too much will make the sev puffy and oily.
* Use the plate with medium-thick holes — larger holes take longer to fry.
* Fry on medium heat — high heat will brown the outside while leaving the inside raw.
* The garlic flavour gets enhanced the next day, so kara sev tastes even better after resting.
* For more crunch, increase rice flour up to 5 tbsp.
* Kara sev stays crisp for about 2 weeks in an airtight container.

Variations:
Pepper Kara Sev, Garlic Kara Sev, Omapodi (plain sev), Mixture Kara Sev

Commonly served with:
Tea, Coffee, Filter Coffee, South Indian Mixture, Chaat Toppings

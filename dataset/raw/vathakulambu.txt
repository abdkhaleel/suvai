Name: Vatha Kuzhambu
Tamil Name: வத்தல் குழம்பு
Category: curry
Sub Category: kuzhambu
Diet: vegetarian, vegan
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Vatha Kuzhambu is a traditional tangy and spicy Tamil Nadu kuzhambu made with sun-dried berries (vathal), tamarind, and sambar powder. It has a unique blend of sour, spicy, and slightly bitter flavors. This comforting dish is a staple in many Tamil homes and pairs excellently with steamed rice.

Ingredients:

* Tamarind (புளி - புளி): 1 lemon sized ball (or 1 tbsp tightly packed)
* Hot water for soaking (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 1 cup
* Gingelly oil (நல்லெண்ணெய் - நல்லெண்ணெய்): 3 tbsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ½ tsp
* Fenugreek seeds (வெந்தயம் - வெந்தயம்): ½ tsp
* Dried red chillies (காய்ந்த மிளகாய் - காய்ந்த மிளகாய்): 1 to 2 (broken)
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 8 to 10
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): 1 pinch
* Manathakkali vathal (மணத்தக்காளி வத்தல் - மணத்தக்காளி வத்தல்): 3 tbsp
* Sambar powder (சாம்பார் பொடி - சாம்பார் பொடி): 1.5 to 2 tbsp
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 2 cups
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Rice flour (அரிசி மாவு - அரிசி மாவு): 1 tsp (optional)
* Jaggery (வெல்லம் - வெல்லம்): ½ tsp (optional)

Steps:

1. Soak tamarind in 1 cup hot water for 20-30 minutes. Squeeze and extract the pulp. Keep aside.
2. Heat gingelly oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them crackle.
3. Add fenugreek seeds, broken red chillies, curry leaves, and asafoetida. Fry on low heat until chillies change color.
4. Add manathakkali vathal and fry on low heat for a few seconds until the color changes slightly. Do not burn.
5. Switch off the heat and add sambar powder. Stir for 1 minute (you can keep on low heat briefly).
6. Add the extracted tamarind pulp and 2 cups water. Stir well.
7. Add salt to taste and bring to a boil on medium-low heat.
8. Simmer for 25-30 minutes until the gravy thickens slightly and oil specks appear on top.
9. Add rice flour and jaggery (if using). Simmer for another 4-5 minutes.
10. Switch off the heat. Let it rest for a few minutes so a layer of oil forms on top. Check seasoning.

Tips:

* Use generous amount of gingelly oil for authentic taste and longer shelf life.
* Fry the vathal and sambar powder on low heat to avoid burning.
* Aged dark tamarind gives better sourness to the kuzhambu.
* This kuzhambu tastes even better the next day as flavors develop.

Variations:
Sundakkai vatha kuzhambu, Mixed vegetable vatha kuzhambu, No vathal version with brinjal or drumstick

Commonly served with:
Steamed rice, Poriyal, Appalam

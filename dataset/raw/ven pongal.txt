Name: Ven Pongal Recipe | Khara Pongal | Ghee Pongal
Tamil Name: Ven Pongal (வெண் பொங்கல்)
Category: breakfast
Sub Category: tiffin
Diet: vegetarian
Difficulty: 
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 3

Description:
Ven Pongal, also known as Khara Pongal or Ghee Pongal, is a popular South Indian savory dish with a soft, porridge-like texture. It is made with rice and yellow moong lentils and flavored with black peppercorns, cumin seeds, asafoetida, curry leaves, and ghee. It is commonly served for breakfast or during festivals.

Ingredients:
- ½ cup rice (100 grams) – any medium to short grained or regular rice
- ¼ cup moong dal (60 grams) – hulled and split mung lentils
- ½ teaspoon cumin seeds – lightly crushed
- ⅛ teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
- 1 inch ginger – chopped or 1 teaspoon heaped chopped ginger
- 3 to 3.25 cups water or add as required
- salt as required

For tempering:
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1 teaspoon black peppercorns whole or crushed (you can also add ½ teaspoon black pepper powder)
- 1 sprig curry leaves or 10 to 12 small to medium curry leaves
- 10 to 12 cashews – whole or halved
- 2 to 3 tablespoons ghee

Steps:
1. Pick moong lentils to remove stones if any. Heat a small pan and roast the moong lentils on low heat till they become fragrant. Do not brown them.
2. Add rice to the roasted moong dal. Rinse both rice and moong dal a couple of times with water. Drain well.
3. Add the drained rice and moong dal to a pressure cooker. Add lightly crushed cumin seeds, asafoetida, chopped ginger and salt as required.
4. Pour 3 to 3.25 cups water. Pressure cook on medium to high heat for 7 to 8 whistles or 11 to 12 minutes.
5. Let the pressure settle down naturally. Open the lid and check consistency. If it looks like pulao, add ½ to 1 cup hot water and mix well. Mash slightly if needed. Keep covered.
6. Heat ghee in a small pan for tempering. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
7. Add cashews and fry till light golden. Then add black peppercorns and curry leaves. Fry till curry leaves become crisp.
8. Pour the entire tempering over the cooked pongal. Mix very well.
9. Cover and let it rest for 5 to 6 minutes so flavors infuse. Serve hot.

Tips:
- Lightly crush cumin seeds before adding for better aroma.
- The final consistency should be soft and porridge-like. Adjust water depending on quality of moong dal.
- You can make it on stove-top in a pan using 3.5 to 4 cups water.
- For more protein, use equal proportions of rice and moong dal.
- Best served fresh. Do not freeze as texture and taste change.

Variations:
- Add mixed vegetables like carrots, green peas, french beans or corn.
- Add oats for healthy oats pongal.
- Can be made with millets or cracked wheat (dalia).

Commonly served with:
coconut chutney, sambar, curd (yogurt), mango pickle, onion gothsu or brinjal gothsu

Name: Panakam
Tamil Name: பானகம்
Category: drinks
Sub Category: panakam
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 0 minutes
Soaking Time: 30 to 40 minutes
Servings: 2
Description:
Panakam, also known as Panagam in Tamil, is a traditional South Indian jaggery-based cooling drink synonymous with the religious festival of Sri Rama Navami. Made by dissolving jaggery in water and flavouring it with lemon juice, dry ginger powder, cardamom and a hint of black pepper, this sacred offering is distributed as prasadam in temples across South India. It is a natural body coolant, aids digestion and makes a refreshing summer beverage when served chilled.

Ingredients:

* Jaggery (வெல்லம் - வெல்லம்): ½ cup (chopped or powdered)
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 2 cups (or as required)
* Lemon juice (எலுமிச்சை சாறு - எலுமிச்சை சாறு): 2 tbsp (or juice of 1 medium lemon)
* Dry ginger powder / Sukku (சுக்கு பொடி - சுக்கு பொடி): ¼ tsp
* Green cardamom powder (பச்சை ஏலக்காய் பொடி - பச்சை ஏலக்காய் பொடி): ⅓ to ½ tsp
* Black pepper powder (மிளகு பொடி - மிளகு பொடி): ⅛ tsp
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): 1 pinch
* Edible camphor / Pachai karpooram (பச்சை கற்பூரம் - பச்சை கற்பூரம்): 1 pinch (optional, for deity offerings)
* Tulsi / Holy basil leaves (துளசி இலை - துளசி இலை): 4 to 5 leaves (optional, for deity offerings)
* Ice cubes (பனிக்கட்டி - பனிக்கட்டி): 3 to 4 (optional)

Steps:

1. In a bowl, take chopped jaggery and 2 cups water.
2. Mix very well and let the jaggery soak for 30 to 40 minutes until completely dissolved.
3. If there are impurities in the jaggery syrup, filter the solution using a fine strainer.
4. Add lemon juice to the dissolved jaggery water.
5. Add dry ginger powder, green cardamom powder, black pepper powder and a pinch of salt.
6. Add a pinch of edible camphor if offering to deities.
7. Mix everything very well until completely dissolved.
8. If offering to deities, add a few holy basil leaves (tulsi) for divine flavour.
9. For serving family and friends, refrigerate for a few hours or add ice cubes.
10. Strain before serving if desired. Serve chilled.

Tips:

* Use good quality or organic powdered jaggery for quick dissolving.
* Hot or warm water helps jaggery dissolve faster.
* Always strain jaggery water to remove impurities.
* A pinch of salt gives balance to the drink instead of being flat sweet.
* Avoid crushing lemon seeds — they can give a bitter taste.
* You can swap lemon with 1 tsp tamarind (soaked in hot water for 20 minutes) for a different variation.
* Panakam can be stored in the fridge for up to a week.
* The colour of panakam depends on the jaggery quality — paagu vellam gives a beautiful golden hue.

Variations:
Tamarind Panakam, Palm Jaggery Panakam, Pepper Panakam, Temple Style Panakam with Tulsi

Commonly served with:
Sri Rama Navami Prasad, Kosambari, Vada Pappu, Neer Mor, Temple Festivals

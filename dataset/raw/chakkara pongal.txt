Name: Sweet Pongal Recipe | Sakkarai Pongal | Chakkara Pongal
Tamil Name: Sakkarai Pongal (சக்கரை பொங்கல்) / Chakkara Pongal
Category: breakfast
Sub Category: tiffin
Diet: vegetarian
Difficulty: 
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Servings: 4

Description:
Sweet Pongal is a creamy, sweet porridge-like dish made with rice, yellow moong lentils and jaggery. It is flavored with green cardamom and finished with ghee, cashews and raisins. Also called Sakkarai Pongal or Chakkara Pongal, it is traditionally prepared during the Pongal festival in South India.

Ingredients:
Main ingredients:
- ½ cup rice (100 grams) – any short to medium grained variety
- ⅓ cup moong dal (85 grams) – husked and split mung lentils (you can also use ¼ cup)
- 3 cups water – for pressure cooking
- ½ cup jaggery chopped and tightly packed (115 to 120 grams) or ½ cup tightly packed jaggery powder
- ½ cup water – for jaggery syrup
- 1 pinch edible camphor – optional
- 5 green cardamoms or ½ teaspoon green cardamom powder
- 1 clove – optional

For frying:
- 4 to 5 tablespoons ghee
- 15 to 18 cashews
- 1 tablespoon golden raisins

Steps:
1. Pick rice and moong dal to remove stones. Roast both together on low heat till aromatic. Do not brown.
2. Rinse roasted rice and moong dal a couple of times. Drain well.
3. Add to pressure cooker with 3 cups water. Pressure cook for 8 to 9 whistles or 11 to 12 minutes on medium heat.
4. Let pressure settle naturally. Open lid, check consistency. If separate like pulao, add ½ to 1 cup hot water. Mash slightly to get khichdi-like texture. Keep covered.
5. Crush seeds of 5 green cardamoms + 1 clove (or use ½ teaspoon cardamom powder).
6. In a pan, dissolve chopped jaggery in ½ cup water on low to medium heat. Strain the syrup if needed and add to the cooked rice-lentil mixture.
7. Add crushed cardamom powder and a pinch of edible camphor. Mix well. Add hot water or milk if too thick.
8. Heat 4 to 5 tablespoons ghee. Fry cashews till light golden, then add raisins and fry till plump.
9. Add the fried cashews, raisins and ghee to the pongal. Mix thoroughly.
10. Serve hot or warm.

Tips:
- Adjust water and whistles depending on quality of moong dal.
- Can be made on stove-top with 4 to 4.5 cups water.
- Pongal thickens as it cools. Add hot water or milk to adjust consistency.
- Store leftovers in fridge for 1-2 days. Do not freeze.

Variations:
- Add milk instead of some water for richer taste.
- Replace jaggery with sugar, coconut sugar or palm sugar.
- Make with cracked wheat (dalia), millets or rolled oats.

Commonly served with:
Served hot as prasadam or festive sweet. Can be garnished with extra fried cashews and raisins.

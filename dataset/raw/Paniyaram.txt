Name: Paniyaram
Tamil Name: குழி பணியாரம்
Category: snacks
Sub Category: paniyaram
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Fermenting Time: 8 to 12 hours
Servings: 18 paniyarams
Description:
Kuzhi Paniyaram is a popular South Indian breakfast and snack made from fermented rice and urad dal batter, seasoned with tempered onions, chillies, ginger and curry leaves, then cooked in a special paniyaram pan with half-round moulds. It has a crispy golden exterior and a soft, spongy interior. Known as Paddu in Karnataka and Guliyappa in Andhra, it is a great way to use leftover idli-dosa batter.

Ingredients:

* Idli dosa batter (இட்லி தோசை மாவு - இட்லி தோசை மாவு): 2 heaped cups (fermented)
* Onion (வெங்காயம் - வெங்காயம்): ½ cup (finely chopped)
* Coriander leaves (கொத்தமல்லி இலை - கொத்தமல்லி இலை): 1 tbsp (finely chopped)
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): as needed (to adjust batter consistency)

For Tempering:
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): 2 tsp + as needed for pan
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ½ tsp
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): 1 tsp (split)
* Chana dal (கடலை பருப்பு - கடலை பருப்பு): 1 tbsp
* Green chilli (பச்சை மிளகாய் - பச்சை மிளகாய்): 1 (finely chopped)
* Ginger (இஞ்சி - இஞ்சி): ½ tsp (finely chopped)
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 tbsp (finely chopped)
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): a pinch

Steps:

1. Heat 2 tsp oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
2. Add urad dal and chana dal. Fry till golden brown.
3. Add green chilli, ginger, curry leaves and asafoetida. Sauté for a few seconds.
4. Add chopped onion and required salt. Sauté until onions turn transparent.
5. Add this tempering to 2 cups of idli dosa batter.
6. Add chopped coriander leaves. Mix well.
7. Add water if needed — the batter should be semi-thick, not too runny.
8. Heat a paniyaram pan (appe pan) on medium heat. Add ¼ tsp oil in each hole.
9. Swirl the pan so oil coats all sides of each cavity evenly.
10. Fill each hole with batter up to ¾th — the paniyarams will rise while cooking.
11. Cook covered on low-medium heat for 3 to 5 minutes until the top looks cooked.
12. Open the lid. When the top looks set and no longer wet, flip each paniyaram using a paniyaram turner or spoon.
13. Cook the other side for 2 minutes until golden brown.
14. Remove and transfer to a plate. Repeat with the remaining batter.
15. Serve hot with coconut chutney, tomato chutney or sambar.

Tips:

* The batter should be semi-thick — too thin and paniyarams will be sticky inside; too thick and they will be dense.
* Swirl the pan after adding oil so all cavities are evenly coated — this prevents sticking.
* Cook covered first so the top steams and cooks through before flipping.
* Use a toothpick to check doneness — it should come out clean without batter sticking.
* A cast iron paniyaram pan gives the best crispy exterior.
* You can add finely chopped carrots, capsicum or grated coconut to the batter for variation.
* For sweet paniyaram, add jaggery syrup, cardamom and coconut to the batter instead of the savoury tempering.
* Paniyarams are best eaten hot. Leftover batter can be refrigerated and used later.

Variations:
Masala Paniyaram, Sweet Paniyaram (Vella Paniyaram), Karupatti Paniyaram, Oats Paniyaram, Rava Paniyaram (Instant)

Commonly served with:
Coconut Chutney, Tomato Chutney, Garlic Chutney, Sambar, Onion Chutney

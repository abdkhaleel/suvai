Name: Urulai Kizhangu Varuval
Tamil Name: உருளைக்கிழங்கு வறுவல்
Category: kootu-poriyal
Sub Category: poriyal
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 3
Description:
Urulai Kizhangu Varuval is a simple and delicious Tamil style potato poriyal recipe. Diced potatoes are slow roasted and skillet fried on a heavy pan preferably cast iron with spices until they are golden brown and crispy. The magic of this spicy potato fry comes from the use of Sambar Powder. It is easy, quick and delicious — a perfect side dish for any variety rice, plain white rice, sambar or rasam.

Ingredients:

* Potatoes / Urulai Kizhangu (உருளைக்கிழங்கு - உருளைக்கிழங்கு): 3 medium, diced into small cubes (~1.5 cups)
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): 2 tsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ½ tsp
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): ½ tsp
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): 1 tsp
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): 1 generous pinch
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 sprig, fresh
* Sambar powder (சாம்பார் பொடி - சாம்பார் பொடி): 1 tsp
* Turmeric powder (மஞ்சள் தூள் - மஞ்சள் தூள்): ¼ tsp
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): ¼ cup (adjust as needed)

Steps:

1. Peel and dice the potatoes into small uniform cubes. Soak them in water to remove excess starch and prevent discoloration.
2. In a cast iron skillet heat 2 teaspoon oil. Add ½ teaspoon mustard seeds, ½ teaspoon cumin seeds, 1 teaspoon urad dal.
3. As the dal turns golden brown, add a generous pinch of asafoetida and a few fresh curry leaves.
4. Next add the diced potatoes (~1.5 cups). Stir fry for a couple of minutes on low flame.
5. Now add 1 teaspoon sambar powder, ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder and salt as needed.
6. Mix well and stir fry for a couple of minutes making sure not to burn the spice powders.
7. Next add about ¼ cup of water and mix it well. Keep it covered and cook on low flame for 4-5 mins.
8. When all the water has been evaporated, continue cooking and stir-frying. Don't cover it with a lid.
9. In the next 5-6 mins, the starch from the potatoes and moisture starts to disappear. Continue stirring occasionally and cook on low flame.
10. As the potato cubes start to turn golden brown, continue cooking on low flame until there is no moisture and the potatoes start to crisp up.
11. As the potatoes turn golden, crispy and devoid of any moisture, without sticking to the pan — remove from heat.
12. Serve hot immediately with any variety rice, plain white rice with sambar, rasam or curd.

Tips:

* Dicing the potatoes into small cubes of even shape and size helps in achieving better results.
* Always soak the potatoes in water after peeling or dicing them — this helps remove the extra starch and makes the fry crispy.
* Always cook the potatoes in a heavy pan preferably cast iron skillet on low flame, stirring them occasionally.
* If you don't have sambar powder, replace it with ½ teaspoon red chilli powder and 1 teaspoon coriander powder.
* Adjust spices as per preference.
* The fry stays crispy when hot and even after cooling down it tastes delicious, making it perfect to pack in lunch boxes.

Variations:
Bangaladumpa Vepudu, Urulai Kizhangu Kara Kari, Nellai Urulai Roast, Potato Podimas

Commonly served with:
Steamed rice, Sambar, Rasam, Curd rice, Variety rice (lemon rice, tamarind rice, etc.)

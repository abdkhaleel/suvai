Name: Ribbon Pakoda
Tamil Name: ரிப்பன் பக்கோடா
Category: snacks
Sub Category: pakoda
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 4 cups
Description:
Ribbon Pakoda, also known as Seeval or Ola Pakoda, is a crunchy, spicy South Indian snack made from a dough of gram flour and rice flour, pressed into ribbon-like strands and deep-fried. It is a festival favourite during Diwali, easy to make and stays crisp for over 10 days when stored properly. The combination of besan and rice flour gives it a unique texture and addictive crunch.

Ingredients:

* Gram flour / Besan (கடலை மாவு - கடலை மாவு): 1 cup
* Rice flour (அரிசி மாவு - அரிசி மாவு): ½ cup
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): 1 tsp
* Red chilli powder (மிளகாய் தூள் - மிளகாய் தூள்): 2 tsp
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Butter / Ghee / Vanaspati (வெண்ணெய் / நெய் - வெண்ணெய் / நெய்): 2 tbsp
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): a pinch
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): as needed
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): for deep frying

Steps:

1. Take gram flour, rice flour, cumin seeds, red chilli powder, salt, asafoetida and butter in a large mixing bowl.
2. Mix well so the butter is evenly incorporated into the flours.
3. Add water slowly and knead into a soft, slightly sticky dough.
4. Heat oil in a kadai for deep frying on medium-high heat.
5. Take the ribbon pakoda mould and fit it into an idiyappam press or murukku maker.
6. Fill the mould with a portion of dough.
7. Press the dough directly into the hot oil in a circular motion, forming ribbon-like strands.
8. Fry on medium-high heat until the sizzling sound subsides and the pakoda turns golden.
9. Flip if needed and fry until evenly crisp.
10. Drain on paper towels.
11. Cool completely and store in an airtight container for more than 10 days.

Tips:

* The dough should be soft and slightly sticky — too dry and it will be hard to press; too wet and it will absorb oil.
* Use the ribbon plate (slotted plate with long rectangular holes) for the correct shape.
* Press directly into the oil while moving in a circular motion to avoid clumping.
* Fry until the "shhh" sound from the oil stops — this indicates the moisture has evaporated and the pakoda is crisp.
* Do not overcrowd the kadai — fry in batches.
* For a peppery version, replace chilli powder with 1 tbsp crushed black pepper.
* Ribbon Pakoda stays crisp for 2 to 3 weeks in an airtight container.

Variations:
Pepper Ribbon Pakoda, Garlic Ribbon Pakoda, Spinach Ribbon Pakoda, Spicy Ribbon Pakoda

Commonly served with:
Tea, Coffee, Filter Coffee, South Indian Mixture

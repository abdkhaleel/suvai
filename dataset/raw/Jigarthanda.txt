Name: Jigarthanda
Tamil Name: ஜிகர்தண்டா
Category: drinks
Sub Category: jigarthanda
Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free
Difficulty: hard
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 1 hour
Freezing Time: 6 hours
Servings: 6
Description:
Jigarthanda is a famous South Indian cooling drink that originated in Madurai, Tamil Nadu. The name literally means "cool heart" (Jigar = heart, Thanda = cool) and it is renowned for its refreshing taste and body-cooling benefits. Traditionally made with reduced milk (khoya), badam pisin (almond gum), nannari syrup and a special khoya ice cream, this rich, layered beverage is a summer favourite. The iconic pinkish ice cream and jelly-like texture make it a unique treat.

Ingredients:

For Khoya / Reduced Milk:
* Full fat milk (முழு பால் - முழு பால்): 2 litres
* Sugar (சர்க்கரை - சர்க்கரை): ½ cup

For Caramel:
* Sugar (சர்க்கரை - சர்க்கரை): 3 tbsp

For Ice Cream Base:
* Cream (க்ரீம் - க்ரீம்): ½ cup
* Vanilla extract (வெண்ணிலா பாகு - வெண்ணிலா பாகு): 1 tsp

For Blooming Badam Pisin:
* Badam pisin / Almond gum (பாதாம் பிசின் - பாதாம் பிசின்): 2 tbsp
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 1 cup

For Serving:
* Nannari syrup (நன்னாரி சர்பத் - நன்னாரி சர்பத்): 4 tbsp
* Regular chilled milk (குளிர்ந்த பால் - குளிர்ந்த பால்): 2 cups
* Crushed ice (பனிக்கட்டி - பனிக்கட்டி): as needed

Steps:

1. Soak badam pisin in 1 cup water overnight or for at least 8 hours. It will bloom into a jelly-like mass. Refrigerate until use.

Making Khoya:
2. Take 2 litres milk in a large pot. Add ½ cup sugar and bring to a full boil.
3. Cook for 20 minutes until reduced by half. Leave about 1½ cups milk in the pot and remove the rest into a bowl. Set aside.
4. Continue boiling the milk in the pot until it thickens into a khoya-like texture. Add 2 more tbsp sugar to help khoya form faster.

Making Caramel:
5. In a separate pan, heat 3 tbsp sugar. Swirl the pan (do not stir) until it melts and turns dark caramel colour.

Making Reduced Milk:
6. Pour the reserved milk into the khoya. Add the caramel and mix well.
7. Remove ⅓ of this mixture into a separate bowl. This is your reduced milk for serving. Refrigerate until needed.

Making Ice Cream:
8. To the remaining reduced milk, add ½ cup cream and 1 tsp vanilla. Mix well.
9. Pour into a freezer-safe container and freeze for 6 hours.
10. Once frozen, scoop into a blender and blend for 2 to 3 minutes until creamy.
11. Pour back into the container and freeze again until solid. Repeat once more for extra creamy ice cream.

Serving:
12. In a tall glass, add 2 to 3 tbsp soaked badam pisin.
13. Add 2 tsp nannari syrup.
14. Pour ¼ cup reduced milk.
15. Top with ½ cup regular chilled milk.
16. Add a scoop of khoya ice cream.
17. Drizzle more nannari syrup on top. Add crushed ice if desired.
18. Mix well and serve immediately.

Quick Method:
1. Boil 500 ml milk with ½ cup condensed milk and caramel for 15 minutes.
2. Mix in cream and vanilla. Freeze and proceed as above.

Tips:

* The ice cream can be made a week in advance and stored in the freezer.
* Soak badam pisin for at least 1 hour, or overnight for best results.
* You can use store-bought nannari syrup to save time.
* Swirl the pan for caramel — never stir or it will crystallize.
* The pinkish colour of the ice cream comes from the caramel added to the milk.
* Some restaurants use vanilla ice cream for convenience, but khoya ice cream gives the authentic taste.
* Consume immediately after mixing — do not let it sit.

Variations:
Quick Jigarthanda (with condensed milk), Jigarthanda with Rose Syrup, Dry Fruit Jigarthanda

Commonly served with:
Summer Evening Snack, Madurai Street Food, Festival Treats

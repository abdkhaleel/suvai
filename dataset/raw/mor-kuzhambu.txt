Name: Mor Kuzhambu
Tamil Name: மோர் குழம்பு
Category: curry
Sub Category: kuzhambu
Diet: vegetarian
Difficulty: medium
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Mor Kuzhambu is a traditional Tamil Nadu yogurt-based curry made with buttermilk, coconut paste, and mild spices. It has a tangy, soothing flavour and is gently simmered with vegetables like ash gourd. This comforting dish is a staple in Tamil homes and is best enjoyed with steamed rice for a light and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

* Tuvar dal (துவரம் பருப்பு - துவரம் பருப்பு): 1 tbsp
* Coriander seeds (தனியா - தனியா): 1 tsp
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): ½ tsp
* Raw rice (பச்சை அரிசி - பச்சை அரிசி): ½ tsp
* Hot water for soaking (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): ¼ cup
* Green chillies (பச்சை மிளகாய் - பச்சை மிளகாய்): 3 to 4
* Ginger (இஞ்சி - இஞ்சி): 1 inch
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 5 to 6
* Grated coconut (தேங்காய் துருவல் - தேங்காய் துருவல்): ¼ cup
* Ash gourd (பூசணிக்காய் - பூசணிக்காய்): 1.5 cups (chopped)
* Turmeric powder (மஞ்சள் தூள் - மஞ்சள் தூள்): ¼ tsp
* Curd (தயிர் - தயிர்): 1 cup (thick and slightly sour)
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 1 cup + as needed
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste

For Tempering:
* Oil or ghee (எண்ணெய் அல்லது நெய் - எண்ணெய் அல்லது நெய்): 1 to 1.5 tbsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ½ tsp
* Fenugreek seeds (வெந்தயம் - வெந்தயம்): ¼ tsp
* Dried red chillies (காய்ந்த மிளகாய் - காய்ந்த மிளகாய்): 1 to 2
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 7 to 8
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): 1 generous pinch

Steps:

1. Rinse tuvar dal, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, and raw rice. Soak in ¼ cup hot water for 20 minutes.
2. Add soaked ingredients along with green chillies, ginger, curry leaves, and grated coconut into a mixer jar. Grind to a smooth paste.
3. Whisk the thick sour curd until smooth. Add the ground paste and 1 cup water. Mix well and set aside.
4. Peel and chop ash gourd into cubes. Cook with 1 cup water and turmeric powder until almost tender.
5. Reduce heat to low, add the buttermilk mixture and salt. Mix gently.
6. Simmer on low heat until the kuzhambu turns frothy and comes to a gentle boil. Do not let it boil vigorously. Turn off the heat.
7. Heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them crackle.
8. Add fenugreek seeds, dried red chillies, curry leaves, and asafoetida. Fry briefly.
9. Pour the tempering over the mor kuzhambu. Cover and let it rest for 5 minutes.
10. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Tips:

* Use slightly sour, full-fat curd for the best taste and to prevent curdling.
* Do not boil the kuzhambu after adding the curd mixture — only gentle simmer is needed.
* Rice or rice flour in the paste helps thicken the gravy and prevents separation.
* You can skip ash gourd or use other vegetables like okra, brinjal, or drumstick.

Variations:
Ash gourd mor kuzhambu, Okra mor kuzhambu, Keerai mor kuzhambu, No-vegetable mor kuzhambu

Commonly served with:
Steamed rice, Idli, Dosa

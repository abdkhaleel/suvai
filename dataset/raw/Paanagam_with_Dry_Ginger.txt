Name: Paanagam with Dry Ginger
Tamil Name: சுக்கு பானகம்
Category: drinks
Sub Category: panakam
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 0 minutes
Soaking Time: 30 to 40 minutes
Servings: 2
Description:
Paanagam with Dry Ginger is a spiced variation of the traditional South Indian jaggery drink, specially prepared during Sri Rama Navami and other summer festivals. The addition of dry ginger powder (sukku) gives it a warm, digestive kick that balances the cooling properties of jaggery and lemon. It is aromatic, refreshing and deeply rooted in South Indian temple tradition as a sacred prasadam.

Ingredients:

* Jaggery (வெல்லம் - வெல்லம்): ½ cup (powdered)
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 2½ cups
* Dry ginger powder / Sukku (சுக்கு பொடி - சுக்கு பொடி): ¼ tsp
* Cardamom (ஏலக்காய் - ஏலக்காய்): 2 (powdered)
* Black peppercorns (மிளகு - மிளகு): 10 (crushed coarsely)
* Lemon juice (எலுமிச்சை சாறு - எலுமிச்சை சாறு): ½ lemon
* Edible camphor / Pachai karpooram (பச்சை கற்பூரம் - பச்சை கற்பூரம்): 1 tiny pinch
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): 1 pinch
* Tulsi / Holy basil leaves (துளசி இலை - துளசி இலை): few (optional)

Steps:

1. Using a hand mortar and pestle, crush 10 peppercorns and 2 cardamom pods to a coarse mixture. Set aside.
2. In a mixing bowl, add 2½ cups water and ½ cup powdered jaggery.
3. Mix well until the jaggery dissolves completely.
4. Add the crushed pepper and cardamom mixture.
5. Add ¼ tsp dry ginger powder, a tiny pinch of edible camphor and a pinch of salt.
6. Add lemon juice from ½ lemon.
7. Add a few tulsi leaves if desired.
8. Mix everything well. Let it rest for at least a few minutes for the flavours to blend.
9. Strain the paanagam before serving.
10. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

Tips:

* Use powdered jaggery for quick dissolving. If using block jaggery, grate it first.
* Freshly crushed pepper and cardamom give more flavour than pre-ground powders.
* Let the paanagam rest for a few minutes before straining — this allows all spices to infuse.
* Adjust lemon juice according to your taste.
* Use paagu vellam (soft, dark reddish-orange jaggery) for a beautiful golden-brown colour.
* Do not add more edible camphor than a tiny pinch — it can overpower the drink.
* Paanagam keeps well in room temperature for 1 day and in the fridge for 2 days.
* The dry ginger adds warmth, making this version especially good during slightly cooler days too.

Variations:
Paanagam with Tamarind, Paanagam with Palm Jaggery, Temple Style Paanagam, Pepper Paanagam

Commonly served with:
Sri Rama Navami Prasad, Kosambari, Vada Pappu, Neer Mor, Temple Festivals, Summer Lunch

Name: Nannari Sarbath
Tamil Name: நன்னாரி சர்பத்
Category: drinks
Sub Category: sarbath
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: medium
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 1 hour
Soaking Time: 5 hours
Servings: 1 cup syrup (makes 8 to 10 glasses)
Description:
Nannari Sarbath is a traditional South Indian cooling drink made from the roots of the Nannari plant (Indian Sarsaparilla). It is a natural body coolant, blood purifier, digestive aid and an excellent thirst quencher during scorching summers. The aromatic roots are boiled, reduced and sweetened into a syrup concentrate that can be stored and diluted with water and lemon juice whenever needed. It is also a key ingredient in the famous Madurai Jigarthanda.

Ingredients:

For Nannari Syrup:
* Nannari roots / Indian Sarsaparilla (நன்னாரி வேர் - நன்னாரி வேர்): 100 grams
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 2 litres
* Sugar (சர்க்கரை - சர்க்கரை): 2 cups
* Lemon juice (எலுமிச்சை சாறு - எலுமிச்சை சாறு): 1 big lemon

For Serving (per glass):
* Nannari syrup (நன்னாரி சர்பத் - நன்னாரி சர்பத்): 3 tbsp
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 1 cup (chilled)
* Lemon juice (எலுமிச்சை சாறு - எலுமிச்சை சாறு): ½ lemon
* Ice cubes (பனிக்கட்டி - பனிக்கட்டி): few
* Soaked sabja seeds (சப்ஜா விதை - சப்ஜா விதை): 1 tsp (optional)

Steps:

1. Crush nannari roots using a mortar and pestle to loosen them and break into 2 to 3 pieces.
2. Remove the white inner stick part from each root and discard it — it is not edible.
3. Wash the roots 2 to 3 times by rubbing in water until all clay and dirt are removed.
4. Dry the roots until there is no moisture.
5. Transfer to a mixer jar and pulse a few times to make a coarse mixture.
6. Add 2 litres water to a mixing bowl. Add the nannari mixture and mix well.
7. Cover and let it rest for a minimum of 5 hours.
8. After 5 hours, transfer to a cooking pot and bring to a boil.
9. Cook on medium flame until the mixture reduces to half in volume.
10. Switch off, cool down, then strain using a double-folded cloth placed on a strainer.
11. Squeeze the cloth well to extract all the juice.
12. Return the strained liquid to the pot. Rinse the pot before adding.
13. Bring to a boil again. When it starts boiling, add 2 cups sugar.
14. Mix well and boil until the syrup is sticky and half-string consistency forms.
15. Switch off. The syrup should be thick but still flowing — like honey.
16. Cool completely, then add juice from 1 big lemon. Mix well.
17. Store in a clean glass bottle. Keeps well at room temperature for a week, or in the fridge for 2 to 3 months.

To Serve:
18. In a glass, add ice cubes, 3 tbsp nannari syrup and lemon juice from ½ lemon.
19. Add 1 cup chilled water. Mix well and serve immediately.

Tips:

* Always remove the white inner stick from nannari roots — it is not edible.
* Use a double-folded muslin cloth to strain and remove even fine root particles.
* Lemon juice is added after cooling to prevent crystallization and increase shelf life.
* The syrup thickens slightly after cooling and storing, so switch off accordingly.
* You can use jaggery instead of sugar for a healthier, caramel-flavoured version.
* Store-bought nannari syrup can be used for convenience, but homemade tastes far superior.
* Add soaked sabja seeds for extra cooling effect and texture.
* You can make nannari popsicles by pouring the sarbath into moulds and freezing overnight.

Name: Meen Kuzhambu
Tamil Name: மீன் குழம்பு
Category: curry
Sub Category: kuzhambu
Diet: non-veg
Difficulty: medium
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Meen Kuzhambu is a traditional tangy and spicy Tamil Nadu fish curry. Fresh fish pieces are cooked in a flavorful tamarind-based gravy with freshly ground spices and coconut. This classic coastal Tamil dish is a favourite in many Tamil homes and is best enjoyed with steamed rice.

Ingredients:

* Fish (மீன் - மீன்): 500 grams (any firm fish like seer fish, mackerel or vanjaram)
* Gingelly oil (நல்லெண்ணெய் - நல்லெண்ணெய்): 4 tbsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ½ tsp
* Fenugreek seeds (வெந்தயம் - வெந்தயம்): ½ tsp
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 2 sprigs
* Shallots / Small onions (சின்ன வெங்காயம் - சின்ன வெங்காயம்): 15 (finely chopped)
* Garlic (பூண்டு - பூண்டு): 8 cloves (crushed)
* Tomato (தக்காளி - தக்காளி): 2 (chopped)
* Tamarind (புளி - புளி): small lemon sized ball
* Turmeric powder (மஞ்சள் தூள் - மஞ்சள் தூள்): ½ tsp
* Red chilli powder (மிளகாய் தூள் - மிளகாய் தூள்): 2 tsp
* Coriander powder (தனியா தூள் - தனியா தூள்): 2 tsp
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste

For Coconut Masala Paste:
* Grated coconut (தேங்காய் துருவல் - தேங்காய் துருவல்): ½ cup
* Fennel seeds (சோம்பு - சோம்பு): 1 tsp
* Shallots (சின்ன வெங்காயம் - சின்ன வெங்காயம்): 5
* Garlic (பூண்டு - பூண்டு): 3 cloves

Steps:

1. Soak tamarind in 1 cup warm water for 15 minutes. Extract juice and discard pulp. Set aside.
2. Grind grated coconut, fennel seeds, shallots, and garlic into a smooth paste. Set aside.
3. Heat gingelly oil in a wide pan or earthen pot. Add mustard seeds and fenugreek seeds. Let them splutter.
4. Add curry leaves, chopped shallots, and crushed garlic. Sauté until shallots turn golden.
5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and mushy.
6. Add turmeric powder, red chilli powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and fry for a minute.
7. Add the extracted tamarind water and bring to a boil.
8. Add the ground coconut masala paste and mix well. Simmer for 5 minutes.
9. Gently add the cleaned fish pieces. Do not stir too much to avoid breaking the fish.
10. Cover and simmer on medium-low heat for 10-12 minutes until fish is cooked and oil floats on top.
11. Check seasoning and adjust if needed. Remove from heat.

Tips:

* Use gingelly oil for authentic taste and aroma.
* Do not overcook the fish — it will become tough.
* Allow the kuzhambu to rest for 15-20 minutes before serving for better flavour.
* This kuzhambu tastes even better the next day.

Variations:
Mackerel Meen Kuzhambu, Seer Fish Kuzhambu, Vendhaya Meen Kuzhambu

Commonly served with:
Steamed rice, Idli, Dosa

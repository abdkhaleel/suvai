Name: Mint Chutney
Tamil Name: புதினா சட்னி
Category: chutney
Sub Category: chutney
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 5 minutes
Servings: 3
Description:
Pudina Chutney is a spicy and refreshing South Indian chutney made with fresh mint leaves, coconut, green chillies and roasted lentils. This vibrant green chutney is a multipurpose condiment that pairs wonderfully with idli, dosa, uttapam, vada or pongal. It can also be used as a spread for sandwiches, wraps and rolls.

Ingredients:

* Fresh mint leaves / Pudina (புதினா இலை - புதினா இலை): ¼ to ½ cup, loosely packed (stalks removed)
* Fresh coconut / grated coconut (தேங்காய் துருவல் - தேங்காய் துருவல்): ½ cup chopped (or 1 cup grated)
* Roasted bengal gram / Pottukadalai (வறுத்த கடலை பருப்பு - வறுத்த கடலை பருப்பு): 1 tbsp (or ¾ tbsp each chana dal + urad dal, dry roasted)
* Green chillies (பச்சை மிளகாய் - பச்சை மிளகாய்): 2 to 3
* Ginger (இஞ்சி - இஞ்சி): ½ inch piece (or 1 to 2 small garlic cloves)
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): ½ tsp
* Lemon juice (எலுமிச்சை சாறு - எலுமிச்சை சாறு): 1 tbsp (or 1 tsp tamarind paste)
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): as needed (for grinding)

For Tempering:
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): 1 tsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ¼ tsp
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): ½ tsp
* Dried red chilli (வற்றல் மிளகாய் - வற்றல் மிளகாய்): 1 (broken, optional)
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): 1 pinch

Steps:

1. Pluck mint leaves from stalks. Rinse them very well in a large bowl of water a few times. Drain completely.
2. If using chana dal and urad dal, dry roast them in a pan on low flame until golden. Add cumin seeds, roast lightly and cool.
3. Add green chillies, coconut, roasted dal, cumin, ginger (or garlic), mint leaves, salt and lemon juice to a blender jar.
4. Blend everything first without water, then add water little by little and blend to a smooth or coarse chutney as desired.
5. Transfer the pudina chutney to a serving bowl.
6. For tempering, heat oil in a small pan. Add dried red chilli, mustard seeds and urad dal. Fry until dal turns golden.
7. Add asafoetida and switch off. Pour over the chutney and mix well.
8. Serve with idli, dosa, uttapam, vada, pongal or as a sandwich spread.

Tips:

* Use fresh mint leaves that are vibrant green. Avoid dark leaves as they can taste bitter after grinding.
* Avoid using stalks and stems of mint — they are too strong and can impart a bitter taste.
* Adding lemon juice not only adds tang but also helps preserve the bright green color.
* Pudina chutney can discolor after a while due to oxidization, so blend just before serving for parties.
* You can also sauté pudina in oil for 2 to 3 minutes before grinding for a different flavour.
* Skip coconut and use roasted peanuts instead for a nutty variation.

Variations:
Mint Coriander Chutney, Mint Peanut Chutney, Mint Coconut Chutney, Yogurt Mint Chutney

Commonly served with:
Idli, Dosa, Uttapam, Vada, Pongal, Upma, Sandwiches, Wraps, Rolls, Pakora

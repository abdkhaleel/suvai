Name: Rava Pongal
Tamil Name: 
Category: breakfast
Sub Category: tiffin
Diet: vegetarian
Difficulty: 
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Servings: 2 to 3

Description:
Rava Pongal is a comforting and delicious South Indian breakfast recipe made with rava (semolina), moong dal, cashews, spices and herbs. It is a quick variation of traditional pongal and stays soft even after cooling, making it good for tiffin box.

Ingredients:
For cooking moong dal:
- 3 tablespoons moong lentils (moong dal)
- ¾ cup water (use ½ to ⅔ cup if moong dal cooks quickly)

Other ingredients:
- ½ cup rava (sooji or cream of wheat) – 90 grams
- 3 tablespoons ghee
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds – whole or slightly crushed
- ½ teaspoon black pepper – slightly crushed (or 1 teaspoon lightly crushed black pepper)
- 1 inch ginger – finely chopped or 1 teaspoon finely chopped ginger
- ⅛ teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
- 12 to 15 cashews
- 1 sprig curry leaves or 10 to 12 curry leaves
- 1.5 cups water
- salt as required

Steps:
1. Pick moong dal to remove stones. Roast moong dal on low heat till aromatic. Do not brown.
2. Rinse roasted moong dal. Pressure cook with ¾ cup water for 6 to 7 whistles (or 2-3 whistles if dal cooks fast). Mash the cooked dal and keep aside.
3. In the same pan, roast ½ cup rava on low heat till fragrant and grains look crisp (about 7-8 minutes). Keep aside.
4. Heat 3 tablespoons ghee. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
5. Add cashews and fry till light golden. Add curry leaves, chopped ginger and crushed black pepper. Stir well.
6. Pour 1.5 cups water. Bring to a rolling boil.
7. Lower heat and add roasted rava in batches, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
8. Add the mashed cooked moong dal and salt. Mix well.
9. Simmer on low heat for a few minutes till rava is cooked and mixture thickens.
10. Serve hot or warm.

Tips:
- Use finer textured rava or Bombay rava.
- You can add grated vegetables or green peas.
- This is a no onion no garlic recipe (onions can be added if desired).
- Stays soft even after cooling, good for lunch box.

Variations:
- Add onions if you like.
- Substitute moong dal with masoor dal or skip lentils (taste will change slightly).

Commonly served with:
coconut chutney, mango pickle, curd (yogurt), sambar

Name: Murukku
Tamil Name: முறுக்கு
Category: snacks
Sub Category: murukku
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: medium
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Murukku is a traditional South Indian deep-fried snack made from rice flour and urad dal flour, shaped into spirals using a murukku press. It is crispy, crunchy and mildly spiced with cumin and asafoetida. A must-have during festivals like Diwali and Gokulashtami, murukku is enjoyed as a tea-time snack and stays fresh for weeks when stored properly.

Ingredients:

* Rice flour (அரிசி மாவு - அரிசி மாவு): 2 cups (roasted, sieved)
* Urad dal flour (உளுந்து மாவு - உளுந்து மாவு): 3 tbsp (roasted, sieved)
* Butter (வெண்ணெய் - வெண்ணெய்): 2 tbsp (softened, at room temperature)
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): 1 tsp
* Sesame seeds (எள் - எள்): 1 tbsp
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): ¼ tsp
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): as needed
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): for deep frying

Steps:

1. Dry roast rice flour in a kadai for 2 minutes on medium flame without changing colour. Sieve and set aside.
2. Dry roast urad dal flour for 1 minute until light golden. Sieve and add to the rice flour.
3. Add cumin seeds, sesame seeds, asafoetida, salt and softened butter to the flour mixture.
4. Rub the butter into the flour with your fingers until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
5. Add water little by little and knead into a smooth, stiff dough. It should not be too soft or too hard.
6. Grease the murukku press with oil and fit the star-shaped plate.
7. Fill the press with a portion of dough.
8. Heat oil in a wide kadai on medium heat. Test by dropping a small piece of dough — it should sizzle and rise steadily.
9. Press the murukku directly into the hot oil in circular motions to form spirals. Do not overcrowd.
10. Fry on medium heat, turning occasionally, until golden and crisp. The sizzling sound will subside when done.
11. Drain on paper towels. Cool completely before storing in an airtight container.

Tips:

* The dough should be stiff but smooth — too soft and murukku will absorb oil; too hard and it will break while pressing.
* Always use a wide kadai so the murukku has space to spread.
* Fry on medium heat — high heat will brown the outside while leaving the inside raw.
* Store-bought rice flour works, but homemade idiyappam flour gives the best results.
* Murukku stays crisp for 2 to 3 weeks in an airtight container.
* You can add 1 tsp red chilli powder for a spicier version.

Variations:
Butter Murukku, Thenkuzhal Murukku, Mullu Murukku, Potato Murukku, Rava Murukku

Commonly served with:
Tea, Coffee, Filter Coffee, South Indian Mixture

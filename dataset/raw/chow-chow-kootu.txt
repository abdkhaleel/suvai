Name: Chow Chow Kadalai Paruppu Kootu
Tamil Name: சௌ சௌ கடலை பருப்பு கூட்டு
Category: kootu-poriyal
Sub Category: kootu
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Chow Chow Kadalai Paruppu Kootu is a simple, mild and comforting Tamil Nadu style kootu made with chayote (chow chow) and chana dal. The vegetable is cooked with lentils and finished with a coconut-cumin paste. This everyday healthy dish is perfect as a side with rice and sambar or rasam.

Ingredients:

* Chow Chow / Chayote (சௌ சௌ / பேரிக்காய் - சௌ சௌ / பேரிக்காய்): 2 big (3.5 to 4 cups when cubed)
* Chana dal / Kadalai paruppu (கடலை பருப்பு - கடலை பருப்பு): ½ cup
* Turmeric powder (மஞ்சள் தூள் - மஞ்சள் தூள்): ¼ tsp
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste

For Grinding:
* Fresh grated coconut (தேங்காய் துருவல் - தேங்காய் துருவல்): ½ cup
* Green chilli (பச்சை மிளகாய் - பச்சை மிளகாய்): 1
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): 1 tsp

For Tempering:
* Coconut oil (தேங்காய் எண்ணெய் - தேங்காய் எண்ணெய்): 1 tsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ½ tsp
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): 1 tsp
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): few

Steps:

1. Wash, peel and chop chow chow into small cubes.
2. In a small pressure cooker, add chana dal at the bottom and add water just enough to cover the dal.
3. Place the chopped chow chow pieces over the dal. Add turmeric powder.
4. Pressure cook for 2 to 3 whistles. Let the pressure release naturally.
5. Meanwhile, grind grated coconut, green chilli and cumin seeds into a smooth paste with very little water.
6. Open the cooker and place it back on low heat. Add salt and bring to a gentle boil.
7. Add the ground coconut paste and mix gently without breaking the vegetable pieces.
8. Let it come to a gentle boil and switch off the heat.
9. Heat coconut oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds, let them crackle.
10. Add urad dal and fry till golden. Add curry leaves and pour the tempering over the kootu. Mix well.

Tips:

* Do not add too much water while pressure cooking to avoid a watery kootu.
* If the kootu is watery, boil on high heat for a few minutes without lid or add a little raw rice while grinding the coconut paste.
* Adding a pinch of jaggery enhances the taste (optional).
* Use coconut oil for authentic flavour.

Variations:
Chow Chow with moong dal, Mixed vegetable kootu, Chow Chow with toor dal

Commonly served with:
Steamed rice, Sambar, Rasam, Vatha Kuzhambu

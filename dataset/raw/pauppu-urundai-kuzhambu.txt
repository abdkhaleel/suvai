Name: Paruppu Urundai Kuzhambu
Tamil Name: பருப்பு உருண்டை குழம்பு
Category: curry
Sub Category: kuzhambu
Diet: vegetarian, vegan
Difficulty: medium
Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 45 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Paruppu Urundai Kuzhambu is a traditional Tamil Nadu lentil dumplings curry. Steamed chana dal dumplings are simmered in a spicy coconut-based tangy gravy. This comforting and protein-rich kuzhambu is often prepared for special lunches, weddings, and festival meals in Tamil homes. It pairs beautifully with hot steamed rice.

Ingredients:

* Chana dal (கடலை பருப்பு - கடலை பருப்பு): ½ cup
* Fennel seeds (சோம்பு - சோம்பு): ¼ tsp (for dumplings) + ½ tsp (for curry)
* Garlic (பூண்டு - பூண்டு): 2 cloves (minced for dumplings) + 4 cloves (crushed for curry)
* Onion (வெங்காயம் - வெங்காயம்): ½ (finely chopped for dumplings) + 1 (chopped for curry)
* Turmeric powder (மஞ்சள் தூள் - மஞ்சள் தூள்): ¼ tsp (for dumplings) + ½ tsp (for curry)
* Green chilli (பச்சை மிளகாய் - பச்சை மிளகாய்): ½ (finely minced)
* Coriander leaves (கொத்தமல்லி இலை - கொத்தமல்லி இலை): 5-6 stalks (chopped)
* Fresh grated coconut (தேங்காய் துருவல் - தேங்காய் துருவல்): ¼ cup (for dumplings) + ¼ cup (for curry)
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): ¼ tsp (for dumplings) + 1 tsp (for curry)
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): 2 tbsp
* Cloves (கிராம்பு - கிராம்பு): 2
* Cinnamon (பட்டை - பட்டை): 1 small piece
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 2 sprigs
* Tomato (தக்காளி - தக்காளி): 3 (pureed)
* Sambar powder (சாம்பார் பொடி - சாம்பார் பொடி): 1 tsp
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): as needed

Steps:

1. Soak chana dal in water for 2 hours. Drain and grind to a coarse paste without adding water.
2. To the ground dal, add fennel seeds, minced garlic, chopped onion, turmeric powder, green chilli, coriander leaves, grated coconut, and salt. Mix well.
3. Shape into small dumplings and steam in an idli vessel for 5 minutes. Set aside.
4. Heat oil in a wide flat pan. Add cloves, cinnamon, fennel seeds, curry leaves, and crushed garlic. Sauté until garlic turns golden.
5. Add chopped onion and sauté until soft.
6. Add pureed tomatoes, salt, turmeric powder, and sambar powder. Cook until oil separates.
7. Grind ¼ cup coconut with ½ cup water into a smooth paste and add to the pan.
8. Add 2 cups water and bring to a full boil. Check seasoning.
9. Gently add the steamed dumplings one by one. Do not stir for the first 5 minutes.
10. Simmer on medium heat for 10-12 minutes. Turn dumplings gently if needed. Remove from heat.

Tips:

* Use a wide flat pan instead of a kadai to prevent dumplings from breaking.
* Do not stir immediately after adding dumplings to avoid breaking them.
* Steam the dumplings only for 5 minutes so they remain soft.
* This kuzhambu tastes even better the next day as the flavours develop.

Variations:
Onion only paruppu urundai kuzhambu, Mixed dal urundai kuzhambu, No coconut version

Commonly served with:
Steamed rice, Appalam

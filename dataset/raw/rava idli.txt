Name: Rava Idli Recipe | Quick Rava Idli (Suji Idli)
Tamil Name: ரவா இட்லி (Rava Idli)
Category: breakfast
Sub Category: tiffin
Diet: vegetarian
Difficulty: 
Prep Time: 35 minutes
Cook Time: 12 minutes
Servings: 10 to 12 idlis

Description:
Rava Idli is a quick, no-fermentation idli made with roasted rava (semolina), curd, and simple spices. These soft, fluffy, and delicious idlis are perfect for a quick breakfast or snack. The addition of grated carrots and fried cashews makes them more nutritious and flavorful. This instant recipe is ready in under an hour and is a great way to enjoy idlis without the usual soaking and grinding process.

Ingredients:
For frying & roasting:
- 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
- 7 to 8 cashews – halved
- ½ teaspoon mustard seeds
- 1 teaspoon chana dal (husked and split bengal gram)
- ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
- 10 curry leaves – chopped
- 1 pinch asafoetida – optional
- 1 teaspoon ginger – finely chopped or 1 inch peeled ginger
- 1 green chilli – finely chopped or ½ to 1 teaspoon, chopped

For batter:
- 2 tablespoons carrots – finely grated, optional
- 2 tablespoons coriander leaves – chopped (cilantro)
- ½ teaspoon salt or add as required
- 1 cup rava – 175 grams (sooji, semolina, cream of wheat)
- ½ cup curd (yogurt)
- ½ to ¾ cup water or add as required
- 1 teaspoon eno (fruit salt) or ¼ teaspoon or 2 to 3 pinches of baking soda

Steps:
1. Heat 2 tablespoons ghee in a pan. Keep the heat to low or medium-low. Add the halved cashews.
2. Stirring often fry the cashews till golden. Remove fried cashews with a slotted spoon draining the ghee and keep aside.
3. To the same pan add mustard seeds and let them begin to splutter. Keep the heat to low or medium-low.
4. When the mustard seeds begin to splutter add the chana dal. Stirring often fry chana dal till they look golden and crunchy.
5. Then add the cumin seeds and fry for 4 to 5 seconds.
6. Now add the chopped curry leaves, asafoetida, finely chopped ginger and green chillies. Mix well and fry for 10 seconds. Keep the heat to a low.
7. Next add rava (cream of wheat, suji, semolina).
8. Mix well and stirring often roast the rava till they become aromatic, change color a bit (but should not get browned or golden) and the grains look separate.
9. Roasting rava will take about 5 to 7 minutes depending on the thickness and heaviness of the pan, and the flame intensity.
10. Once the rava is well roasted, keep the pan aside. Let the rava become warm.
11. To the roasted rava, add finely grated carrots, chopped coriander leaves and ½ teaspoon salt or add as required.
12. Next add curd (yogurt) and water as required. Depending on the quality of rava and the thickness of curd, you can adjust the water from ½ to ¾ cup.
13. Mix very well and let the batter rest covered for 20 minutes. The rava idli batter is neither thick nor thin in consistency and has a medium consistency.
14. Brush oil or ghee on the idli pan moulds. Place a fried halved cashew in the center of the moulds.
15. Also heat 2 to 2.5 cups water in a pot. Keep a small length trivet stand or a rack in the pot. Heat the water till it comes to a boil.
16. After 20 minutes, add 1 teaspoon eno (fruit salt) or baking soda to the batter. Mix gently but quickly.
17. Immediately pour the batter into the greased idli moulds.
18. Place the idli stand in the steamer or pressure cooker (without whistle). Steam on medium heat for 10 to 12 minutes.
19. Check with a toothpick – it should come out clean. Switch off the heat and let it stand for 2 minutes.
20. Remove the idli stand carefully. Demould the rava idlis and serve hot.

Tips:
- Use the finer variety of rava and not the coarser or bigger ones. You can use Bombay rava or chiroti rava.
- The rava has to be roasted well in ghee so that they do not become sticky after steaming.
- Eno (fruit salt) and baking soda are the leavening agents used in the recipe. You can add either of them. Do make sure they are fresh and within their shelf period. If they are not fresh or active, they won’t leaven the batter resulting in hard rava idli.
- You can skip them, but the rava idli won’t have a fluffy texture. They will be soft though with a slight denseness in the texture.
- If you do not have an idli pan, you can use a cake pan or bowl for the batter and steam. Once steamed well, then slice in wedges or cubes.

Variations:
- Add grated vegetables like carrots, beans, or peas for more nutrition and color.
- Skip cashews or add roasted peanuts instead.
- You can make plain rava idli without any vegetables or tempering.

Commonly served with:
coconut chutney, sambar, tomato chutney, onion chutney

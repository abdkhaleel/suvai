Name: Therattipal
Tamil Name: திரட்டிப்பால்
Category: sweets
Sub Category: therattipal
Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 3 to 4 hours (traditional) / 20 minutes (quick)
Servings: 6
Description:
Therattipal, also known as Palkova or Thirattipaal, is a traditional South Indian milk sweet made by slowly reducing milk until it thickens to a grainy, fudge-like consistency, then sweetened with sugar and flavoured with cardamom. The traditional method takes 3 to 4 hours of patient simmering, but a quick version using condensed milk can be made in just 20 minutes. It is a favourite for Krishna Jayanthi, Diwali and other festivals, with a rich, caramelized milk flavour that is deeply satisfying.

Ingredients (Traditional Method):

* Whole milk / Full fat milk (முழு பால் - முழு பால்): 8 cups (2 litres)
* Sugar (சர்க்கரை - சர்க்கரை): 1 cup
* Cardamom powder (ஏலக்காய் பொடி - ஏலக்காய் பொடி): 1 tsp

Ingredients (Quick Method):

* Condensed milk (கண்டென்ஸ்டு பால் - கண்டென்ஸ்டு பால்): 1 tin (400g)
* Milk (பால் - பால்): ½ cup
* Curd / Yogurt (தயிர் - தயிர்): ¼ cup (fresh, not sour)
* Ghee (நெய் - நெய்): 1 to 2 tbsp

Steps (Traditional Method):

1. Pour 8 cups milk into a wide, heavy-bottomed vessel. Heat on medium flame.
2. Let the milk come to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
3. To prevent milk from boiling over, place a small stainless steel plate inside the vessel.
4. Let the milk reduce slowly, stirring frequently (every 5 minutes) to prevent sticking.
5. After 2 to 3 hours, the milk will reduce to a mud-like consistency — about ¼ of the original volume.
6. Add 1 cup sugar and stir well. The mixture will liquefy slightly.
7. Continue cooking until it comes to a semi-solid, thick consistency.
8. Switch off when still slightly runny — it will solidify as it cools.
9. Add cardamom powder, mix well and let cool.

Steps (Quick Method):

1. To a kadai, add 1 tin condensed milk and ½ cup milk. Keep flame on low.
2. Mix well until blended. Let it start to boil.
3. Once boiling, add ¼ cup fresh curd. The mixture will start to curdle, giving a grainy texture.
4. Cook on low flame, stirring continuously to avoid burning.
5. As it thickens, add 1 tbsp ghee. Keep scraping milk solids from the sides.
6. Add another tbsp ghee if needed. Cook until the colour turns golden.
7. The mixture will leave the sides of the kadai and come together like a mass.
8. Switch off when thick but still slightly flowing — it thickens further on cooling.
9. Cool for a few minutes and serve.

Tips:

* Use a wide, heavy-bottomed pan for the traditional method to prevent burning and sticking.
* Whole milk gives the richest flavour, but 2% milk also works — it just takes longer to reduce.
* Raw sugar gives a more golden colour compared to white sugar.
* Do not let the mixture dry out completely — switch off when still slightly goey.
* For the quick method, use fresh curd that is not sour, or the sweet will taste tangy.
* Ghee adds richness and prevents the therattipal from sticking to the pan.
* You can slightly grease the pan with water before starting to prevent sticking.
* Therattipal stays good for 3 to 4 days at room temperature and up to a week refrigerated.

Variations:
Palkova (plain version), Milk Peda, Doodh Peda, Kalakand, Milk Cake

Commonly served with:
Krishna Jayanthi Prasad, Diwali Sweets, Festival Offerings, Tea, Coffee

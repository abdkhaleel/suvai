Name: Adhirasam
Tamil Name: அதிரசம்
Category: sweets
Sub Category: adhirasam
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: hard
Prep Time: 3 hours
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Resting Time: 1 day
Servings: 20 adhirasams
Description:
Adhirasam is a traditional deep-fried South Indian sweet made from raw rice flour and jaggery syrup, flavoured with cardamom and dry ginger. It has a unique texture — crispy on the edges and pillowy soft inside. A Diwali essential in Tamil Nadu, making perfect adhirasam requires patience and practice, especially in getting the jaggery syrup consistency and rice flour moisture right. The dough is rested for a day before frying, which develops its characteristic flavour and texture.

Ingredients:

* Raw rice / Maavu pacharisi (அரிசி - அரிசி): 1½ cups (IR20 or IR64 variety preferred)
* Jaggery / Paagu vellam (வெல்லம் - வெல்லம்): 1 cup (powdered)
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): ½ cup (for syrup)
* Dry ginger powder / Sukku (சுக்கு பொடி - சுக்கு பொடி): ⅛ tsp
* Cardamom powder (ஏலக்காய் பொடி - ஏலக்காய் பொடி): ⅛ tsp
* Sesame seeds (எள் - எள்): 1 tbsp (optional)
* Ghee (நெய் - நெய்): 1 tbsp (for greasing)
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): for deep frying

Steps:

1. Wash raw rice and soak in water for 3 hours.
2. Drain and spread the rice on a clean white cotton cloth for 30 minutes. The rice should still have moisture — when pressed, a few grains should stick to your hand. Do not let it dry completely.
3. Grind the rice in a mixer in 2 batches to a semi-fine powder. It should be slightly coarse, not as fine as store-bought flour.
4. Sieve the flour. Return coarse remains to the mixer and grind again with the next batch.
5. Add sesame seeds, dry ginger powder and cardamom powder to the flour. Mix well, cover with a moist cloth and set aside.
6. Crush jaggery, add ½ cup water and heat until dissolved. Strain to remove impurities.
7. Heat the strained jaggery syrup again on medium flame, stirring occasionally. Keep checking consistency by pouring drops into a bowl of water.
8. At the correct stage, the syrup will not dissolve in water. You will be able to gather it with your fingers and form a loose, jelly-like soft ball that slides easily from your hand. This is soft ball consistency — switch off immediately.
9. Add the rice flour to the hot syrup little by little, mixing continuously without lumps. The dough will be slightly loose.
10. Transfer to an airtight container and rest at room temperature for 1 day. The dough will thicken and become firm.
11. The next day, add 1 tsp ghee and knead the dough once. Pinch and roll into lemon-sized balls.
12. Grease a banana leaf or zip-lock cover with ghee. Pat each ball into a slightly thick round disc.
13. Heat oil on low-medium heat. Slide the patted adhirasam into the oil.
14. Let it rise up, then pour hot oil over it so it puffs up. Flip and pour oil again.
15. Fry until deep red in colour. Use two ladles to gently squeeze out excess oil.
16. Drain on 2 to 3 layers of kitchen tissues. Cool and store.

Tips:

* Choosing the correct raw rice (maavu pacharisi) and jaggery (paagu vellam) is the first key to authentic adhirasam.
* The rice must have some moisture after drying — completely dry rice gives rubbery adhirasam.
* Do not use a very fine sieve — slightly bigger holes give the right coarse flour texture.
* Jaggery syrup consistency is the most critical step. Do not let it pass soft ball stage.
* Resting the dough for at least 1 day is mandatory — it ferments slightly and develops flavour.
* Fry on low-medium heat so the inside cooks through while the outside turns crisp.
* If dough is too tight, sprinkle a little hot water and knead. If too loose, add 1 to 2 tbsp idiyappam flour.
* Adhirasam stays good for 2 to 3 days at room temperature. For longer storage, refrigerate and steam lightly before serving.

Variations:
Sugar Adhirasam (with white sugar), Karupatti Adhirasam (with palm jaggery), Coconut Adhirasam

Commonly served with:
Diwali Sweet Platter, Festival Prasad, Tea, Coffee

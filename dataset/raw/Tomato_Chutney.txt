Name: Tomato Chutney
Tamil Name: தக்காளி சட்னி
Category: chutney
Sub Category: chutney
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Tomato Chutney or Thakkali Chutney is a very popular South Indian side dish made with ripe tomatoes, chillies and garlic. It is tangy, mildly sweet and spicy — a perfect pair for idli, dosa, paniyaram, masala dosa, or even as a spread for sandwiches. Cooking the tomatoes down until mushy and then tempering gives this chutney a deep, rich flavour.

Ingredients:

* Tomatoes (தக்காளி - தக்காளி): 4 to 5 large, chopped (or 2 cups)
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): 1 tbsp (for sautéing) + 4 tbsp (for tempering)
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): 2 tbsp
* Dry red chillies (வற்றல் மிளகாய் - வற்றல் மிளகாய்): 5
* Garlic (பூண்டு - பூண்டு): 3 to 4 cloves (optional)
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Sugar / Jaggery (சர்க்கரை / வெல்லம் - சர்க்கரை / வெல்லம்): 1 tsp (optional)

For Tempering:
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): 1 tsp
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 sprig
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): ¼ tsp

Steps:

1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan. Add urad dal and dry red chillies. Sauté till golden.
2. Add chopped tomatoes, salt and sugar. Mix well.
3. Cover and simmer for 5 to 10 minutes until tomatoes are completely cooked and mushy.
4. Cool the mixture completely, then transfer to a blender and puree till smooth.
5. For tempering, heat 4 tbsp oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
6. Add curry leaves and asafoetida. Let them sizzle.
7. Pour the tomato puree into the tempering oil. Add a splash of water if needed.
8. Cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the chutney thickens and oil separates on the sides.
9. Remove from heat and serve hot or at room temperature.

Tips:

* Use ripe red tomatoes which are a little sour for the best taste.
* Use gingelly oil for maximum authentic flavour.
* Urad dal can be substituted with chana dal, flax seeds or sesame seeds.
* Garlic can be added for extra depth of flavour.
* Cooking till oil separates helps preserve the chutney longer without refrigeration.
* Store in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Variations:
Onion Tomato Chutney, Tomato Coconut Chutney, Tomato Mint Chutney, Spicy Tomato Chutney

Commonly served with:
Idli, Dosa, Paniyaram, Masala Dosa, Uttapam, Pesarattu, Parathas, Sandwiches, Pakora

Name: Neer Mor
Tamil Name: நீர் மோர்
Category: drinks
Sub Category: buttermilk
Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 5 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Neer Mor, literally translating to "water buttermilk," is a refreshing spiced buttermilk drink from South India. Made by diluting fresh thick curd with water and flavouring it with green chillies, cumin seeds, ginger, curry leaves and coriander leaves, it is a staple during hot summers and an essential offering during Sri Rama Navami and Sumangali Pooja. It aids digestion, cools the body and is a healthy, low-calorie alternative to aerated drinks.

Ingredients:

* Thick curd / Yogurt (தயிர் - தயிர்): 1 cup (fresh, not sour)
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 2½ to 4 cups (chilled)
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): ½ tsp
* Green chilli (பச்சை மிளகாய் - பச்சை மிளகாய்): 1 (finely chopped)
* Ginger (இஞ்சி - இஞ்சி): 1 tsp (finely chopped)
* Coriander leaves (கொத்தமல்லி இலை - கொத்தமல்லி இலை): 1 tbsp (finely chopped)
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 tbsp (finely chopped)
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): 1 tiny pinch

For Tempering:
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): ½ tsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ½ tsp
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 sprig
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): ¼ tsp

Steps:

1. To a mixing bowl, add 1 cup fresh thick curd.
2. Add 2½ cups chilled water. Whisk vigorously using a hand churner or whisk until nice and frothy. You can also use a mixie with churner attachment.
3. Add cumin seeds (you can crush them for more flavour), finely chopped green chilli, ginger, coriander leaves and salt.
4. Mix everything well.
5. For tempering, heat ½ tsp oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
6. Add curry leaves and asafoetida. Fry for a few seconds until leaves are crisp.
7. Pour the tempering into the buttermilk and mix well.
8. Serve chilled or at room temperature. You can strain before serving if preferred.

Tips:

* Use fresh thick curd that is not sour for the best taste.
* Neer Mor is meant to be watery — adjust water quantity as per your liking.
* You can add raw curry leaves instead of tempering them.
* Blend all ingredients along with curd and water in a blender, rest for a few minutes, then strain and serve.
* Add raw cumin seeds or use them for tempering along with other ingredients.
* Neer Mor is best consumed fresh, but can be refrigerated for up to 2 days.
* For a creamier version, use less water (2 cups instead of 4).

Variations:
Sambharam (Kerala style), Chaas (North Indian style), Masala Buttermilk, Spiced Buttermilk with Mint

Commonly served with:
Sri Rama Navami Prasad, South Indian Meals, Summer Lunch, Mid-morning Snack, Kosambari, Panakam

Name: Mysore Pak
Tamil Name: மைசூர் பாக்
Category: sweets
Sub Category: barfi
Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free
Difficulty: medium
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Servings: 12 pieces
Description:
Mysore Pak is a legendary South Indian melt-in-the-mouth sweet made with just three main ingredients — gram flour (besan), sugar and ghee. Originating from the royal kitchens of Mysore, this fudgy, soft barfi has a rich golden colour and an irresistible aroma of roasted besan and pure ghee. It is a must-make during Diwali and special occasions, and though it looks simple, getting the perfect soft texture requires attention to sugar syrup consistency and timing.

Ingredients:

* Gram flour / Besan (கடலை மாவு - கடலை மாவு): 1 cup
* Sugar (சர்க்கரை - சர்க்கரை): 1 cup
* Ghee (நெய் - நெய்): 1 cup (divided)
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): ¼ cup
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): ¾ cup (optional, mixed with ghee for softer texture)

Steps:

1. Grease a square or rectangular plate generously with ghee and set aside.
2. Heat 1 tsp ghee in a non-stick pan. Add besan flour and roast on low-medium flame for 3 to 4 minutes until a nice aroma comes. Do not brown.
3. Sieve the roasted besan once to remove lumps and ensure fine texture.
4. In a bowl, mix ¾ cup ghee with the roasted besan to form a smooth, slightly pouring consistency mixture. Set aside for 15 minutes.
5. In a non-stick pan, add 1 cup sugar and ¼ cup water. Heat on medium flame, stirring until sugar dissolves.
6. Bring to a boil and cook until one-string consistency is reached — when you take the syrup between thumb and index finger, a single string should form.
7. Add the besan-ghee mixture to the sugar syrup. Mix well until combined.
8. Add the remaining ¼ cup ghee in intervals, stirring continuously. Keep the flame on low.
9. The mixture will first stick to the pan while scraping with the ladle. Keep stirring and adding ghee little by little.
10. At one stage, the mixture will come together as a ball without sticking at all. It will not be sticky anymore. This is the perfect stage — switch off immediately.
11. Pour the mixture into the greased plate. Do not flatten — it will spread beautifully by itself.
12. While still hot, cut into squares or diamonds using a greased knife.
13. Let it cool completely. The ghee that oozes out will be fully absorbed as it cools.
14. Remove pieces gently and store in an airtight container.

Tips:

* The two critical steps are: checking one-string consistency of sugar syrup and the final Mysore Pak consistency.
* Switch off at the exact moment the mixture leaves the pan and forms a non-sticky ball — even a minute late will make it hard.
* Keep the flame on low after adding besan to prevent burning.
* The colour of Mysore Pak depends on the besan flour — deeper yellow besan gives a richer colour.
* Ghee may ooze out slightly at the final stage — this is normal and will be absorbed on cooling.
* Add ghee in portions, not all at once — the mixture absorbs ghee quickly.
* Use a non-stick pan for easier handling and to prevent sticking.
* Mysore Pak stays well at room temperature for 5 days and in the fridge for 2 weeks.

Variations:
Ghee Mysore Pak, Milk Mysore Pak, Hard Mysore Pak (Crispy version), Chocolate Mysore Pak, Kesar Mysore Pak

Diwali Sweets Platter, Tea, Coffee, Festival Gift Boxes

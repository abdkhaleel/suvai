Name: Red Chilli Chutney
Tamil Name: சிவப்பு மிளகாய் சட்னி
Category: chutney
Sub Category: chutney
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 5 minutes
Servings: 3
Description:
Red Chilli Chutney is a Tamil Nadu style spicy condiment made with dry red chillies, coconut, roasted chana dal, ginger, garlic and tamarind. The tamarind adds a pleasant sourness that balances the heat from the chillies. Finished with a cracking tempering of mustard, cumin and curry leaves, this chutney is perfect with idli, dosa, uttapam, medu vada and bonda.

Ingredients:

* Fresh grated coconut (தேங்காய் துருவல் - தேங்காய் துருவல்): ½ cup
* Roasted chana dal (வறுத்த கடலை பருப்பு - வறுத்த கடலை பருப்பு): ¼ cup
* Dry red chillies (வற்றல் மிளகாய் - வற்றல் மிளகாய்): 1 to 2 (broken, seeds removed)
* Ginger (இஞ்சி - இஞ்சி): 1 tsp (chopped)
* Garlic (பூண்டு - பூண்டு): 2 to 3 small cloves (chopped)
* Tamarind (புளி - புளி): 1 tsp
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): ½ cup (for grinding, or as needed)

For Tempering:
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): 1 tbsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): 1 tsp
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): 1 tsp
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): 1 pinch
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 sprig (10 to 12 leaves)

Steps:

1. Take grated coconut, roasted chana dal, dried red chillies, chopped ginger, garlic, tamarind and salt in a grinder jar.
2. Add ½ cup water and blend to a smooth and fine consistency.
3. Transfer the chutney to a heat-proof bowl.
4. Heat oil in a small pan on low flame. Add mustard seeds and let them crackle.
5. Add cumin seeds and fry till they splutter.
6. Add asafoetida and curry leaves. Fry for a few seconds until curry leaves become crisp.
7. Switch off heat and pour the entire tempering over the chutney.
8. Mix well and serve with idli, dosa, uttapam, medu vada, bonda or pakoda.

Tips:

* Use Kashmiri or Byadgi red chillies for a milder, brighter coloured chutney.
* If using hot and pungent red chillies, reduce the quantity.
* Skip asafoetida to make this chutney fully gluten-free.
* Adjust the consistency by adding less or more water. Be mindful of salt when changing consistency.
* You can add a few curry leaves while grinding for a unique taste.
* Adding sambar onions/shallots while grinding gives an extra layer of flavour.
* This chutney is best consumed immediately after preparing.

Variations:
Red Coconut Chutney, Kara Chutney, Spicy Red Chutney, Red Chilli Garlic Chutney

Commonly served with:
Idli, Dosa, Uttapam, Medu Vada, Mysore Bonda, Pakoda, Rava Idli, Masala Dosa

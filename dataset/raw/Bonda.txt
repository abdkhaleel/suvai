Name: Bonda
Tamil Name: போண்டா
Category: snacks
Sub Category: bonda
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Soaking Time: 2 hours
Servings: 20 bondas
Description:
Mysore Bonda, also known as Ulundu Bonda, is a popular South Indian snack made from urad dal batter, chopped coconut, black pepper and fresh herbs. These round, fluffy fritters are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. A favourite in Tamil Nadu, Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh, they are traditionally served with coconut chutney and make a perfect evening snack with coffee or tea.

Ingredients:

* Urad dal / White whole urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): ½ cup
* Fresh coconut bits (தேங்காய் துண்டுகள் - தேங்காய் துண்டுகள்): 3 tbsp (chopped)
* Black peppercorns (மிளகு - மிளகு): 2 tsp (crushed coarsely)
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): ¼ tsp
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Rice flour (அரிசி மாவு - அரிசி மாவு): 2 tsp
* Coriander leaves (கொத்தமல்லி இலை - கொத்தமல்லி இலை): 2 tbsp (chopped)
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 sprig (chopped)
* Green chilli (பச்சை மிளகாய் - பச்சை மிளகாய்): 1 (optional, chopped)
* Ginger (இஞ்சி - இஞ்சி): ½ inch piece (finely chopped, optional)
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): ½ tsp (optional)
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): for deep frying

Steps:

1. Rinse urad dal well and soak in water for 2 to 3 hours (or overnight for best results).
2. Drain all water completely. Transfer soaked dal to a blender or grinder.
3. Add 4 to 5 tbsp water in parts and grind to a smooth, fluffy and thick batter. Scrape sides as needed.
4. Transfer the batter to a large mixing bowl. Whisk briskly with a spoon for 2 to 3 minutes to aerate the batter.
5. Add rice flour, crushed black pepper, chopped ginger, green chilli, asafoetida, chopped coriander leaves, curry leaves, chopped coconut bits, cumin seeds and salt.
6. Mix everything very well.
7. Heat oil in a kadai on medium heat. Test by dropping a pinch of batter — it should rise steadily.
8. Grease your hand with water. Take a small portion of batter and shape into a smooth ball.
9. Carefully drop the ball into the hot oil. Lower the flame and cook slowly.
10. Fry until golden on all sides, turning occasionally. The inside must cook through.
11. Drain on paper towels. Serve hot with coconut chutney.

Tips:

* Use fresh urad dal, not aged or old — old dal takes longer to grind and won't yield fluffy bondas.
* Grind the batter with very little water and use ice water for the best fluffy texture.
* Keep the batter thick and flowing — thin batter makes flat bondas that absorb too much oil.
* Adding rice flour or fine rava helps make the bonda crispier.
* If the batter is too loose, refrigerate for 1 hour before frying.
* Fry on medium heat — too hot and the outside will brown while the inside stays raw; too low and they will absorb oil.
* Bondas are best eaten hot or warm. Store in an airtight container for a few hours on the same day.

Variations:
Aloo Bonda (Potato-filled), Goli Baje (Maida version), Egg Bonda, Masala Bonda

Commonly served with:
Coconut Chutney, Tomato Chutney, Garlic Chutney, Tea, Coffee, Filter Coffee

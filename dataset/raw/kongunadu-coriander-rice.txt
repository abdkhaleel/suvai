Name: Kongunadu Village Style Coriander Rice
Tamil Name: கோதமல்லி சாதம்
Category: rice
Sub Category: variety rice
Diet: vegetarian
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 5 minutes
Servings: 2
Description:
Kongunadu Village Style Coriander Rice (Kothamalli Sadam) is a quick, aromatic rice dish from the Kongu region of Tamil Nadu, made with a coarse spice powder of carom seeds, chillies, and pepper. It is a traditional medicinal lunch recipe valued for its digestive properties and simple village-style preparation using leftover rice. This no-onion-garlic recipe is perfect for busy days and highlights everyday Tamil kitchen herbs.

Ingredients:

* Rice (அரிசி - அரிசி): 1 cup (boiled)
* Carom seeds (ஓமம் - ஓமம்): 1 ½ tbsp
* Dry red chilli (காய்ந்த மிளகாய் - காய்ந்த மிளகாய்): 2
* Peppercorns (மிளகு - மிளகு): 4
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 sprig
* Garlic (பூண்டு - பூண்டு): 2 cloves
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): a pinch
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Gingelly oil (நல்லெண்ணெய் - நல்லெண்ணெய்): 1 ½ tbsp

Steps:

1. Dry roast carom seeds, dry red chilli, and peppercorns one by one and let them cool.
2. Coarsely grind the roasted ingredients without water using a mixer or mortar-pestle.
3. Heat gingelly oil in a pan; add curry leaves, garlic, and asafoetida; sauté.
4. Add the freshly ground powder mixture and sauté briefly.
5. Add boiled rice and salt; mix well.
6. Pour 1 tbsp gingelly oil, mix thoroughly, and remove from heat.

Tips:

* Roast spices on low flame to prevent burning and retain flavour.
* Use leftover cooled rice for better grain separation.
* Serve hot with papad for a complete simple meal.

Variations:
Lemon coriander rice, mint coriander rice, coconut coriander rice

Commonly served with:
Appalam, papad

Name: Paal Payasam
Tamil Name: பால் பாயசம்
Category: sweets
Sub Category: payasam
Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 90 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Paal Payasam is a classic South Indian rice pudding made by slow-cooking rice in milk until the milk caramelizes and turns creamy. This old-fashioned, labour-of-love dessert is the sweet ending to any traditional South Indian lunch. The milk is cooked for nearly one and a half hours, reducing to a rich, light caramel colour with the rice completely melted into the milk. It is simple, divine and deeply comforting.

Ingredients:

* Full fat milk / Whole milk (முழு பால் - முழு பால்): 1 litre
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 1½ litres
* Raw rice (அரிசி - அரிசி): 3 tbsp
* Sugar (சர்க்கரை - சர்க்கரை): ½ cup
* Cardamom (ஏலக்காய் - ஏலக்காய்): 3 (powdered fine)
* Ghee (நெய் - நெய்): 2 tsp
* Cashew nuts (முந்திரி பருப்பு - முந்திரி பருப்பு): 7 to 8 (broken)

Steps:

1. Take a wide, deep-bottomed heavy pan. Add 1 litre milk and 1½ litres water.
2. Bring to a boil on medium-high flame. Once boiling, add washed raw rice.
3. Keep cooking on medium-high flame. Because of the large quantity of water, the milk will not bubble over. Stir once every 5 minutes.
4. The milk mixture will start evaporating and reducing in volume. After 30 to 40 minutes, it would have reduced by approximately half.
5. After about 1 hour and 15 minutes, the mixture will become creamy in colour and thicker in consistency. Most of the liquid will have evaporated.
6. Add ½ cup sugar and mix well. Let it boil for 2 minutes.
7. Switch off the flame and add powdered cardamom. Mix well to combine.
8. Heat 2 tsp ghee in a small pan. Fry broken cashews on low flame until slightly golden.
9. Add the fried cashews to the payasam and mix.
10. Serve warm. The payasam thickens further on cooling.

Alternative Pressure Cooker Method:
1. Roast 2 tbsp raw rice with 1 tsp ghee until fragrant. Coarsely powder the rice (just break it).
2. Grease a large pressure cooker with a few drops of ghee.
3. Add 4 cups milk and bring to a boil. Add the powdered rice.
4. Pressure cook for 1 whistle, then keep on the lowest possible flame for 10 minutes.
5. Open the cooker, add ½ cup sugar and boil on low flame for another 10 minutes until thick.
6. Add cardamom powder and fried cashews. Serve.

Tips:

* Use a wide, deep, heavy-bottomed pan to prevent the milk from boiling over and scorching.
* The large quantity of water prevents the milk from bubbling over — do not reduce the water.
* Stir once every 5 minutes — constant stirring is not needed.
* Use full-fat milk for the creamiest result. Low-fat milk may curdle.
* The milk slowly caramelizes during the long cooking, giving the payasam its unique light creamy colour and rich flavour.
* If using the pressure cooker method, grease the cooker well to prevent burnt bottom and overflow.
* Add a small stainless steel plate inside the vessel while boiling to prevent milk from boiling over.
* Paal Payasam is best served warm. Refrigerate leftovers and reheat gently before serving.

Variations:
Paramannam (with jaggery), Semiya Payasam (with vermicelli), Paalada Payasam, Arisi Thengai Payasam

Commonly served with:
South Indian Lunch (Sadya), Onam Sadya, Wedding Feasts, Temple Prasad, Appalam (Papad)

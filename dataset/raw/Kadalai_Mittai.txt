Name: Kadalai Mittai
Tamil Name: கடலை மிட்டாய்
Category: sweets
Sub Category: mittai
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: medium
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 12 to 15 balls
Description:
Kadalai Mittai, also known as Kadalai Urundai or Verkadalai Urundai, is a traditional South Indian peanut candy made with just two main ingredients — roasted peanuts and jaggery. These crunchy, sweet peanut balls are a nostalgic childhood treat, commonly found in street-side stores across Tamil Nadu. They are also made during Karthigai Deepam festival as neivedhyam. The key to perfect kadalai mittai lies in getting the jaggery syrup to hard ball candy stage.

Ingredients:

* Peanuts / Groundnuts / Verkadalai (வேர்க்கடலை - வேர்க்கடலை): 1 cup (raw or unsalted roasted)
* Jaggery (வெல்லம் - வெல்லம்): ½ cup
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): ¼ cup
* Ghee (நெய் - நெய்): 1 tsp (for greasing hands, or coconut oil for vegan)

Steps:

1. If using raw peanuts, roast them until aromatic and golden. You can roast in oven (350°F for 15-20 mins), air fryer (325°F for 5-7 mins) or on stovetop (low flame, shaking pan constantly).
2. Let peanuts cool completely. Rub between your palms or in a kitchen towel to remove skins.
3. Blow away the skins and keep skinned peanuts in a bowl. You can crush them slightly into halves if preferred.
4. Heat jaggery and ¼ cup water in a pan. Let the jaggery melt completely.
5. Strain to remove impurities, then heat the syrup again on medium flame.
6. Keep checking consistency by dropping a little syrup into a bowl of cold water. At the correct stage, you can gather it with your fingers and form a hard ball that holds its shape without disintegrating. The ball should make a noise when dropped on a plate.
7. Immediately switch off the flame. Add the roasted peanuts to the hot syrup.
8. Mix quickly and thoroughly using a wooden spoon until all peanuts are evenly coated.
9. Let it cool slightly until you can handle it. Grease your palms with ghee.
10. Take small portions and roll into tight balls while still hot. Work quickly — once cooled, the mixture becomes too hard to shape.
11. Place the balls on a plate in a single layer to prevent sticking. Let them cool completely.
12. Once completely dry and cool, store in an airtight container.

Tips:

* Use roasted and skinless peanuts for best results. Smaller Spanish peanuts work very well.
* Use fresh, good-quality peanuts — old peanuts have a stale flavour.
* Getting the jaggery syrup to hard ball stage takes practice. Use a candy thermometer (300°F / 149°C) if available.
* Work quickly when shaping — the mixture hardens as it cools. If it becomes too hard, slightly heat the plate to soften.
* Keep mixing the peanuts in the syrup while cooling to ensure even coating — otherwise jaggery settles at the bottom.
* If the kadalai mittai turns out soft and chewy, the syrup was not cooked to hard ball stage.
* Let the balls air dry completely before storing — overnight drying is ideal.
* Kadalai mittai stays well at room temperature for a long time in an airtight container.

Variations:
Kadalai Chikki (bar form), Mixed Nut Brittle, Sesame Peanut Balls, Karupatti Kadalai Mittai

Commonly served with:
Karthigai Deepam Prasad, Tea, Coffee, Festival Snack, Street-side Treat

Name: Bajji
Tamil Name: பஜ்ஜி
Category: snacks
Sub Category: bajji
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Bajji is a popular South Indian tea-time snack where vegetables are dipped in a spiced gram flour batter and deep-fried until golden and crisp. Common vegetables used include raw banana, potato, onion rings, chilli bajji and brinjal. The batter is light, crispy and mildly spiced — perfect for rainy evenings with a hot cup of tea or coffee.

Ingredients:

* Gram flour / Besan (கடலை மாவு - கடலை மாவு): 1 cup
* Rice flour (அரிசி மாவு - அரிசி மாவு): ¼ cup
* Red chilli powder (மிளகாய் தூள் - மிளகாய் தூள்): 1 tsp
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): ½ tsp
* Baking soda / Cooking soda (சமையல் சோடா - சமையல் சோடா): ½ tsp
* Ginger powder (இஞ்சி பொடி - இஞ்சி பொடி): ½ tsp (optional)
* Garlic powder (பூண்டு பொடி - பூண்டு பொடி): ½ tsp (optional)
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): as needed (for batter)
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): for deep frying

For Frying:
* Raw banana / Potato / Onion / Chilli (வாழைக்காய் / உருளைக்கிழங்கு / வெங்காயம் / மிளகாய் - வாழைக்காய் / உருளைக்கிழங்கு / வெங்காயம் / மிளகாய்): 2 to 3 (sliced)

Steps:

1. Sift gram flour and rice flour together into a large bowl.
2. Add red chilli powder, asafoetida, baking soda, ginger powder, garlic powder and salt.
3. Mix all dry ingredients well.
4. Add water little by little to make a smooth, thick batter. The consistency should coat the back of a spoon.
5. Heat oil in a kadai on medium heat.
6. Slice the vegetables into thin, even pieces. For chilli bajji, make a slit and remove seeds.
7. Dip each vegetable slice into the batter, coating it completely.
8. Gently drop the coated vegetable into the hot oil. Fry a few at a time — do not overcrowd.
9. Fry on medium heat, turning occasionally, until golden and crisp on all sides.
10. Drain on paper towels. Serve hot with coconut chutney or tomato chutney.

Tips:

* The batter should be thick enough to coat the vegetables well — too thin and it won't stick; too thick and it will be doughy.
* Slice vegetables evenly so they cook uniformly.
* For extra crispy bajji, add 1 tbsp hot oil to the batter before frying.
* Adding rice flour to the batter makes the bajji extra crispy.
* Fresh ginger-garlic paste can be added to the batter instead of powders for better flavour and to prevent gastric issues.
* Serve immediately while hot for the best crispiness.

Variations:
Vazhakkai Bajji (Raw Banana), Potato Bajji, Onion Bajji, Chilli Bajji, Brinjal Bajji, Cauliflower Bajji

Commonly served with:
Tea, Coffee, Filter Coffee, Coconut Chutney, Tomato Chutney, Onion Chutney

Name: Onion Chutney
Tamil Name: வெங்காய சட்னி
Category: chutney
Sub Category: chutney
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Servings: 3
Description:
Onion Chutney or Vengaya Chutney is a South Indian side dish made with onions, spices and herbs. It is quick, delicious and hot — with a perfect balance of sweet, sour and spicy flavours. Sautéed onions are blended with roasted lentils, peanuts, tamarind and chillies, then finished with a simple tempering. A great alternative when you run out of tomatoes or coconut.

Ingredients:

* Onions (வெங்காயம் - வெங்காயம்): 1 cup cubed (2 medium) or shallots
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): 1½ to 2 tbsp (for frying) + ½ tbsp (for tempering)
* Peanuts (வேர்க்கடலை - வேர்க்கடலை): 2 tbsp
* Chana dal (கடலை பருப்பு - கடலை பருப்பு): ¾ tbsp (optional)
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): ¾ tbsp (optional)
* Garlic (பூண்டு - பூண்டு): 2 cloves
* Dried red chillies (வற்றல் மிளகாய் - வற்றல் மிளகாய்): 3 to 4 (less spicy variety)
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 sprig (optional)
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): ½ tsp
* Tamarind (புளி - புளி): 1 tsp (soaked in ½ cup water)
* Jaggery (வெல்லம் - வெல்லம்): 1 tsp (optional)
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): ½ tsp (or to taste)
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): ½ cup (as needed)

For Tempering:
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ¼ tsp
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): ¼ tsp (optional)
* Dried red chilli (வற்றல் மிளகாய் - வற்றல் மிளகாய்): ½ (broken)
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 10 leaves
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): 1 pinch

Steps:

1. Soak tamarind and jaggery in ½ cup water. Set aside.
2. Heat 1½ tbsp oil in a pan. Add chana dal, urad dal, peanuts and dried red chillies.
3. Fry on medium heat, stirring often, until lightly golden and aromatic.
4. Add garlic and cubed onions. Fry for 3 to 4 minutes until onions are half done.
5. Add cumin seeds and curry leaves. Sauté until onions are light golden and soft.
6. Cool completely. Transfer to a blender jar along with salt.
7. Squeeze soaked tamarind and filter through a small sieve. Add to the blender.
8. Blend until smooth, adding more water as needed to reach desired consistency.
9. Taste test and adjust salt. Transfer to a serving bowl.
10. For tempering, heat ½ tbsp oil. Add mustard seeds, urad dal and dried red chilli.
11. When dal turns light golden, add curry leaves and asafoetida. Turn off heat.
12. Pour tempering over the chutney and mix well. Serve with idli, dosa or snacks.

Tips:

* Sautéing the onions well until they lose raw flavour and begin to caramelize is the key step.
* Use shallots for a more traditional and sweeter flavour.
* Do not reduce oil — onions need enough oil to fry well and develop flavour.
* Keep the flame medium while sautéing onions to prevent lentils from burning.
* This chutney can be stored in the fridge for up to 48 hours.
* You can add coriander leaves or mint leaves after frying onions for a fresh twist.

Variations:
Raw Onion Chutney, Onion Tomato Chutney, Onion Peanut Chutney, Onion Coconut Chutney

Commonly served with:
Idli, Dosa, Uttapam, Pesarattu, Parathas, Sandwiches, Pakora, Cutlets, Bonda

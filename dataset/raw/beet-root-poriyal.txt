Name: Beetroot Poriyal
Tamil Name: பீட்ரூட் பொரியல்
Category: kootu-poriyal
Sub Category: poriyal
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 3
Description:
Beetroot Poriyal is a colourful and nutritious Tamil Nadu style stir-fried beetroot dish. Fresh beetroots are cooked with coconut and basic spices. This healthy and mildly sweet poriyal is a regular side dish in Tamil meals and pairs wonderfully with rice, sambar, and rasam.

Ingredients:

* Beetroot (பீட்ரூட் - பீட்ரூட்): 2 medium (250-300 grams)
* Gingelly oil / Coconut oil (நல்லெண்ணெய் அல்லது தேங்காய் எண்ணெய் - நல்லெண்ணெய் அல்லது தேங்காய் எண்ணெய்): 2 tbsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): 1 tsp
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): 1 tsp
* Green chilli (பச்சை மிளகாய் - பச்சை மிளகாய்): 1 (chopped)
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 sprig
* Turmeric powder (மஞ்சள் தூள் - மஞ்சள் தூள்): ¼ tsp
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): 1 pinch
* Grated coconut (தேங்காய் துருவல் - தேங்காய் துருவல்): ¼ to ⅓ cup
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): ¼ to ⅓ cup (as needed)

Steps:

1. Peel and finely chop the beetroots into small cubes.
2. Heat oil in a kadai. Add mustard seeds and let them crackle.
3. Add urad dal and fry until golden brown.
4. Add chopped green chilli and curry leaves. Sauté for a few seconds.
5. Add chopped beetroot, turmeric powder, asafoetida, and salt. Mix well.
6. Sprinkle water, cover and cook on low-medium heat until beetroot is tender (stir occasionally).
7. Once cooked and water evaporates, add grated coconut.
8. Mix thoroughly and cook for 2 minutes on low heat.
9. Remove from heat and serve hot.

Tips:

* Chop beetroot into small uniform pieces for even and faster cooking.
* Adjust water carefully — beetroot releases some moisture while cooking.
* Fresh grated coconut gives the best flavour and texture.
* You can add a pinch of sugar or jaggery for extra mild sweetness.

Variations:
Beetroot Carrot Poriyal, Grated Beetroot Poriyal, Beetroot with moong dal

Commonly served with:
Steamed rice, Sambar, Rasam, Curd rice

Name: Seedai
Tamil Name: உப்பு சீடை
Category: snacks
Sub Category: seedai
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: medium
Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 1 hour
Servings: 6 cups
Description:
Uppu Seedai is a popular South Indian crunchy snack made with rice flour, urad dal flour, coconut and cumin, specially prepared during Krishna Jayanthi (Gokulashtami). These tiny, round, deep-fried balls are crispy and mildly spiced. Making perfect seedai requires attention to moisture control to prevent them from bursting in oil.

Ingredients:

* Rice flour (அரிசி மாவு - அரிசி மாவு): 1 cup (homemade or store-bought, very fine)
* Urad dal flour (உளுந்து மாவு - உளுந்து மாவு): 1 tbsp
* Butter (வெண்ணெய் - வெண்ணெய்): 2 tbsp (soft, at room temperature)
* Grated coconut (தேங்காய் துருவல் - தேங்காய் துருவல்): 1 tbsp (finely ground)
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): 1 tsp
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): ¼ tsp
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): ½ cup (or as needed)
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): for deep frying
* Sesame seeds (எள் - எள்): 1 tsp (optional)

Steps:

1. Sift rice flour and urad dal flour separately at least twice to ensure they are perfectly smooth.
2. Dry roast rice flour in a kadai for 2 minutes on low heat. Remove to a bowl.
3. Dry roast urad dal flour for 1 minute until light golden. Add to the same bowl.
4. Grind grated coconut in a blender without water until fine. Dry roast in a kadai until moisture evaporates. Add to the flour mixture.
5. Add salt, cumin seeds, asafoetida, sesame seeds and soft butter.
6. Rub the butter into the flour mixture with your fingers until evenly incorporated.
7. Sprinkle water little by little and knead into a soft, stiff dough. Do not add too much water.
8. Take small portions of dough and roll very gently into tiny smooth balls without applying pressure.
9. Spread the balls on a dry cloth and let them sit for 30 minutes to remove excess moisture.
10. Heat oil for deep frying. Do a test batch first — drop a few balls and step away. If they don't burst, proceed.
11. Fry the seedai on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until light golden and crisp.
12. Drain on paper towels. Cool completely before storing.

Tips:

* The ratio of rice flour to urad dal flour is crucial — 11:1 is the traditional ratio.
* Sifting and roasting flours and coconut removes moisture, which is the main cause of seedai bursting.
* Roll the balls gently without pressure — tight balls will burst while frying.
* Always do a test fry before frying the full batch. Stand back when dropping them in oil.
* Drying the rolled balls on a cloth for 30 minutes is essential to prevent bursting.
* If you are scared of bursting, prick each ball with a needle before frying.
* Fry on medium heat — very high heat will colour the outside but leave the inside raw.
* Seedai stays crisp for 1 to 2 weeks in an airtight container.

Variations:
Vella Seedai (sweet), Karupatti Seedai, Uppu Seedai with Chana Dal

Commonly served with:
Tea, Coffee, Filter Coffee, South Indian Mixture, Festival Prasad

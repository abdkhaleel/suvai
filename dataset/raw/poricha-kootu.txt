Name: Poricha Kootu
Tamil Name: பொரிச்ச கூட்டு
Category: kootu-poriyal
Sub Category: kootu
Diet: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Poricha Kootu is a traditional Tamil Nadu style dry kootu made with mixed vegetables and moong dal, finished with a roasted coconut-pepper-cumin spice mix. It has a unique earthy flavour and aroma. This healthy and comforting dish is usually served as a side with rice, sambar and rasam in everyday Tamil meals.

Ingredients:

* Mixed vegetables (சுண்டல் காய்கறிகள் - சுண்டல் காய்கறிகள்): 2 to 2.5 cups (chow chow, carrot, beans, peas, potato, ash gourd)
* Moong dal (பாசிப்பருப்பு - பாசிப்பருப்பு): ⅓ cup
* Turmeric powder (மஞ்சள் தூள் - மஞ்சள் தூள்): ¼ tsp
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): to taste
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): as needed

For Spice Paste:
* Grated coconut (தேங்காய் துருவல் - தேங்காய் துருவல்): ½ cup
* Dried red chillies (காய்ந்த மிளகாய் - காய்ந்த மிளகாய்): 2
* Black peppercorns (மிளகு - மிளகு): ½ tsp
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): 1 tsp
* Rice (அரிசி - அரிசி): ½ tsp (raw rice)

For Tempering:
* Coconut oil or gingelly oil (தேங்காய் எண்ணெய் அல்லது நல்லெண்ணெய் - தேங்காய் எண்ணெய் அல்லது நல்லெண்ணெய்): 2 tsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ½ tsp
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): 1 tsp
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 sprig
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): 1 pinch

Steps:

1. Rinse moong dal and pressure cook with ¾ cup water and turmeric powder for 2-3 whistles until soft. Mash lightly and keep aside.
2. Chop all vegetables into small cubes.
3. In a pan, add the chopped vegetables with little water and salt. Cook until vegetables are soft but not mushy.
4. Dry roast dried red chillies, black pepper, cumin seeds and raw rice until aromatic. Cool slightly.
5. Grind the roasted ingredients along with grated coconut into a coarse paste. Add very little water if needed.
6. Add the cooked moong dal and ground coconut paste to the cooked vegetables. Mix gently.
7. Simmer on low heat for 3-4 minutes until everything is well combined. Adjust consistency if needed.
8. Heat oil in a small tadka pan. Add mustard seeds, let them crackle.
9. Add urad dal and fry until golden. Add curry leaves and asafoetida. Pour this tempering over the kootu.
10. Mix well and serve hot.

Tips:

* Use a mix of vegetables for better taste and nutrition.
* Do not add too much water while cooking vegetables to keep the kootu dry.
* Roast the spices on low flame to avoid burning.
* This kootu tastes best when served slightly warm.

Variations:
Snake gourd poricha kootu, Cabbage poricha kootu, Beans poricha kootu

Commonly served with:
Steamed rice, Sambar, Rasam, Curd rice

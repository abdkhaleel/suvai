Name: Kesari
Tamil Name: கேசரி
Category: sweets
Sub Category: kesari
Diet: vegetarian, gluten-free
Difficulty: easy
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Servings: 3
Description:
Kesari, also known as Rava Kesari or Sheera, is a beloved South Indian sweet dish made from roasted semolina (rava), sugar, ghee and flavoured with cardamom and saffron. It is soft, aromatic and has a beautiful golden-yellow hue. Kesari is a staple at temples, weddings, festivals and special occasions, often served as prasadam. It can be made plain or with variations like pineapple, banana or carrot.

Ingredients:

* Rava / Semolina / Sooji (ரவா - ரவா): ½ cup
* Sugar (சர்க்கரை - சர்க்கரை): 1 cup
* Water (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 1½ cups
* Ghee (நெய் - நெய்): ¼ cup
* Oil (எண்ணெய் - எண்ணெய்): 1 tbsp (optional, for softness)
* Cardamom powder (ஏலக்காய் பொடி - ஏலக்காய் பொடி): ¼ tsp
* Saffron strands (குங்குமப்பூ - குங்குமப்பூ): 5 to 6 strands
* Food colour (உணவு வண்ணம் - உணவு வண்ணம்): 1 tiny pinch (kesari colour, optional)
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): ⅛ tsp
* Cashew nuts (முந்திரி பருப்பு - முந்திரி பருப்பு): 15
* Raisins (திராட்சை - திராட்சை): 10

Steps:

1. Heat ¼ cup ghee in a thick-bottomed kadai. Add cashews and fry until golden.
2. Add raisins and fry until they puff up and turn golden. Remove both and set aside.
3. In the same ghee, add rava in a sprinkled manner. Roast on low flame for 3 to 5 minutes until aromatic and slightly golden. Do not brown.
4. Meanwhile, in another pan, heat 1½ cups water with salt, saffron strands and a pinch of food colour. Bring to a rolling boil.
5. Add the boiling water to the roasted rava. Stir continuously on medium flame to avoid lumps.
6. The mixture will become porridge-like. Cook covered on low flame for 5 to 7 minutes, stirring in between.
7. After 6 minutes, the rava should be fully cooked and soft. Give it a quick stir.
8. Add 1 cup sugar. Stir well to break any lumps that form. The mixture will loosen and become watery.
9. Keep stirring on medium flame until it starts to thicken again.
10. Cook partially covered for 2 minutes, then stir until the mixture starts to leave the sides of the pan.
11. Add cardamom powder, fried cashews, raisins and 1 tbsp oil (optional). Oil helps keep kesari soft even after cooling.
12. Give a quick mix and switch off. The kesari should still be slightly flowing in consistency — it will set as it cools.
13. Serve warm with a dollop of ghee.

Tips:

* Do not compromise on ghee — it is the main ingredient that makes kesari tasty and aromatic.
* Always add sugar only after the rava is fully cooked, or the texture will be affected.
* Adding a tablespoon of oil at the end keeps kesari soft for hours without hardening.
* If you are a beginner, cook the entire process on low flame to avoid burning.
* Use a thick-bottomed kadai or non-stick pan for best results.
* For a more gooey kesari, add 3¼ to 3½ cups water instead of 3 cups.
* You can skip food colour and use only saffron for a natural light yellow hue.

Variations:
Pineapple Kesari, Banana Kesari, Carrot Kesari, Milk Kesari, Mango Kesari, Rava Kesari with Coconut

Commonly served with:
Breakfast (Idli, Dosa, Pongal), Temple Prasad, Wedding Feasts, Festival Offerings

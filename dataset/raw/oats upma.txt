Name: Oats Upma Recipe (Indian Savory Oats)
Tamil Name: ஓட்ஸ் உப்புமா (Oats Uppuma)
Category: breakfast
Sub Category: tiffin
Diet: vegetarian
Difficulty: 
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 3

Description:
Oats Upma is an easy and delicious breakfast recipe made with quick-cooking oats, mixed vegetables and simple spices. It is a lighter, fiber-rich twist on traditional upma. This wholesome dish is vegan and gets ready in just 30 minutes. It is perfect for busy mornings.

Ingredients:
For roasting oats:
- 1 cup (100 grams) quick cooking oats
- ½ teaspoon oil – any neutral oil

Other ingredients:
- ¼ cup onions – tightly packed, finely chopped
- ¼ cup carrots – finely chopped
- ¼ cup french beans – finely chopped
- ¼ cup green peas – fresh or frozen
- 7 to 8 curry leaves – large or 10 to 12 medium to small curry leaves
- 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1 teaspoon chana dal – optional
- 1 teaspoon urad dal – optional
- 8 to 10 cashews or roasted peanuts
- ½ teaspoon ginger – finely chopped
- 1 or 2 green chillies – chopped
- 1 to 2 tablespoons coriander leaves – chopped (cilantro)
- 1.25 cups water or add as required
- 2 tablespoons oil – any neutral oil or coconut oil
- salt as required

Steps:
1. Heat ½ teaspoon oil in a pan. Add 1 cup quick cooking oats.
2. Stir and roast on a low heat until the oats become crisp. Keep stirring continuously so that the oats do not get browned.
3. Remove oats in a plate or tray and set aside.
4. In the same pan, heat 2 tablespoons oil. Add mustard seeds. Stir and fry until the mustard seeds crackle.
5. Then add cumin seeds, chana dal & urad dal (optional) and cashews. Stir and fry until the chana dal gets browned.
6. Add finely chopped onions. Stir and sauté until the onions soften and turn translucent.
7. Next add finely chopped ginger, chopped green chillies and curry leaves. Stir and sauté for half a minute.
8. Next add finely chopped carrots, finely chopped french beans and green peas. Stir and sauté for 2 minutes.
9. Add water. Cover the pan with its lid. On a medium heat simmer and cook until the veggies are almost done (about 8 to 9 minutes).
10. Add the roasted oats. Stir and mix lightly. Add salt as required. Mix very well.
11. Cover the pan again and on a low to medium-low heat, steam for 7 to 8 minutes.
12. Once done, add chopped coriander leaves. Stir and serve oats upma drizzled with some lemon or lime juice.

Tips:
- Use any type of oats, but adjust water and cooking time accordingly.
- Lightly roast oats before cooking to prevent stickiness.
- Add your choice of vegetables for better taste and nutrition.
- Cook in coconut oil, ghee, or any neutral oil.
- Recipe can be easily doubled or tripled.

Variations:
- Customize with vegetables like capsicum, cauliflower, broccoli, baby corn, cabbage, corn kernels etc.
- Skip urad dal or chana dal if you don’t have them.
- Skip cashews or use roasted peanuts instead.
- Skip green chilies and add black pepper or red chili flakes instead.
- Use rolled oats, steel-cut oats or instant oats (adjust water accordingly).

Commonly served with:
drizzled with some lemon or lime juice or with a side of coconut chutney. Can be served with masala chai or filter coffee.

Name: Sambar
Tamil Name: சாம்பார்
Category: curry
Sub Category: kuzhambu
Diet: vegetarian, vegan
Difficulty: medium
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 40 minutes
Servings: 4
Description:
Sambar is a flavorful South Indian lentil stew made with toor dal, mixed vegetables, tamarind, and aromatic sambar powder. It is a daily staple in Tamil Nadu households, known for its comforting taste and versatility. This wholesome dish pairs perfectly with rice, idli, dosa, vada, or pongal and provides complete nutrition with protein and vegetables.

Ingredients:

* Toor dal (துவரம் பருப்பு - துவரம் பருப்பு): ¾ cup
* Water to cook dal (தண்ணீர் - தண்ணீர்): 2 cups
* Mixed vegetables (onion/shallots, drumstick, okra, carrot, tomato) (காய்கறிகள் - காய்கறிகள்): as needed
* Tamarind (புளி - புளி): 1 to 2 tbsp (lemon sized)
* Jaggery (வெல்லம் - வெல்லம்): 1 tbsp (optional)
* Salt (உப்பு - உப்பு): ¾ tbsp (to taste)
* Turmeric powder (மஞ்சள் தூள் - மஞ்சள் தூள்): ¼ tsp
* Red chilli powder (மிளகாய் தூள் - மிளகாய் தூள்): ½ to ¾ tsp (optional)
* Coriander leaves (கொத்தமல்லி இலை - கொத்தமல்லி இலை): ¼ cup (chopped)

For Sambar Powder:
* Dried red chilies (காய்ந்த மிளகாய் - காய்ந்த மிளகாய்): 4 to 5
* Chana dal (கடலை பருப்பு - கடலை பருப்பு): 1 tbsp
* Urad dal (உளுந்து பருப்பு - உளுந்து பருப்பு): 1 tsp
* Coriander seeds (தனியா - தனியா): 1 tbsp
* Fenugreek seeds (வெந்தயம் - வெந்தயம்): ¼ to ½ tsp
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): ½ tsp

For Tempering:
* Ghee or oil (நெய் அல்லது எண்ணெய் - நெய் அல்லது எண்ணெய்): 1 tbsp
* Mustard seeds (கடுகு - கடுகு): ½ tsp
* Cumin seeds (சீரகம் - சீரகம்): ½ tsp (optional)
* Fenugreek seeds (வெந்தயம் - வெந்தயம்): 1 pinch
* Dried red chilies (காய்ந்த மிளகாய் - காய்ந்த மிளகாய்): 1 to 2
* Curry leaves (கறிவேப்பிலை - கறிவேப்பிலை): 1 sprig
* Asafoetida (பெருங்காயம் - பெருங்காயம்): ⅛ tsp

Steps:

1. Wash toor dal and pressure cook with 2 cups water until soft and mushy (2-4 whistles). Mash well and set aside.
2. While dal cooks, dry roast ingredients for sambar powder (red chilies, chana dal, urad dal, coriander seeds, fenugreek, cumin) and grind to a fine powder.
3. Rinse and chop vegetables (shallots/onion, drumstick, okra, carrot, tomato).
4. Heat 1 tsp oil in a pot; sauté onions for 1 minute, add all vegetables and sauté for 2 minutes.
5. Add 4 cups water and cook vegetables until tender.
6. Soak tamarind in hot water, squeeze and extract juice (filter if needed).
7. Add turmeric powder, homemade sambar powder, salt, and tamarind water to the cooked vegetables; simmer for 3-5 minutes.
8. Add mashed dal, mix well, adjust consistency with hot water if needed, and bring to a gentle boil. Add chopped coriander leaves.
9. For tempering: Heat ghee/oil, add mustard seeds, cumin, fenugreek, red chilies, curry leaves, and asafoetida. Pour over the sambar.
10. Simmer for 2-3 minutes and serve hot.

Tips:

* Use fresh homemade sambar powder for the best aroma and taste.
* Cook vegetables until soft so their flavors blend well into the stock.
* Add tamarind only after vegetables are cooked to ensure proper tenderness.
* For storage, refrigerate without tempering and add fresh tempering when reheating.

Variations:
Arachuvitta sambar, Idli sambar, Hotel style sambar, Vegetable sambar

Commonly served with:
Rice, Idli, Dosa, Medu Vada, Pongal
